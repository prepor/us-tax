# Pennsylvania (PA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6% statewide; 7% in Allegheny County (6% state + 1% local); 8% in Philadelphia (6% state + 2% local)

Sources: [Pennsylvania Sales, Use and Hotel Occupancy Tax](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/sales-use-and-hotel-occupancy-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in Pennsylvania annual gross sales (includes taxable and nontaxable sales, across all channels)
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2019
- **Collection timing:** Collection obligation begins April 1 of the year following the calendar year in which the $100,000 threshold was crossed.
- **Official nexus information:** [Online Retailers - PA Department of Revenue](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/sales-use-and-hotel-occupancy-tax/online-retailers), [Tax Obligations for Online Retailers](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/tax-obligations-for-online-retailers)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through myPATH (my Pennsylvania Tax Hub) by completing the Pennsylvania Online Business Tax Registration. Apply for a Sales, Use and Hotel Occupancy Tax License.
- **Registration portal:** [myPATH](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/mypath) / [Register My Business for Taxes](https://www.pa.gov/services/revenue/register-my-business-for-taxes)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register for a Pennsylvania sales tax license.

Sources: [Register My Business for Taxes](https://www.pa.gov/services/revenue/register-my-business-for-taxes), [myPATH](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/mypath)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Most tangible personal property is subject to the 6% state sales tax (plus applicable local tax).
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food (unprepared/groceries):** Exempt. Most food purchased for off-premises consumption is not taxable. Ready-to-eat/prepared food is taxable.
  - **Clothing:** Exempt. Most clothing for everyday wear is exempt from sales tax. However, the following clothing-related items are **taxable** per 61 Pa. Code §53.1: formal day or evening apparel, fur articles (where fur is the chief value component), and clothing/equipment designed specifically for sports or athletic activity (e.g., athletic uniforms, bathing suits, protective gear).
  - **Prescription and non-prescription drugs/medicine:** Exempt.
  - **Textbooks:** Exempt.
  - **Residential heating fuels:** Exempt (oil, electricity, gas, coal, firewood).
  - **Computer services:** Exempt.
- **Official exemptions information:** [Sales, Use and Hotel Occupancy Tax](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/sales-use-and-hotel-occupancy-tax), [Retailer's Information Guide REV-717 (PDF)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/revenue/documents/formsandpublications/formsforbusinesses/sut/documents/rev-717.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Only two jurisdictions impose local sales tax:
  - **Allegheny County:** 1% local tax (total 7%)
  - **Philadelphia:** 2% local tax (total 8%)
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers must collect the applicable local tax for deliveries made into Allegheny County or Philadelphia.

Sources: [Pennsylvania Sales, Use and Hotel Occupancy Tax](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/sales-use-and-hotel-occupancy-tax), [Online Retailers](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/sales-use-and-hotel-occupancy-tax/online-retailers)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned by the Department of Revenue based on your tax liability. Frequency options include monthly, quarterly, and semi-annual.
- **Filing deadlines:** For monthly filers, prepayments are due by the 20th of the current month and returns are due by the 20th of the following month. See the [2026 PA Sales Tax Filing Schedule (REV-819) (PDF)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/revenue/documents/formsandpublications/formsforbusinesses/sut/documents/2026_rev-819.pdf) for complete due dates.
- **Filing portal:** [myPATH](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/mypath) or TeleFile at 1-800-748-8299

Sources: [2026 Tax Due Date Reference Guide (PDF)](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/revenue/documents/formsandpublications/otherforms/documents/dpo-05.pdf), [myPATH](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/mypath)
