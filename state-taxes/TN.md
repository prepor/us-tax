# Tennessee (TN) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 7% (general rate); 4% on food and food ingredients
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 9.548% (state 7% + local up to 2.75%). Local rates vary by county/city.

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in sales to Tennessee customers during the previous 12-month period
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Tennessee uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2019 (originally with a $500,000 threshold, which was later reduced to $100,000)
- **Official nexus information:** [Out of State Dealers & Marketplace Facilitators](https://www.tn.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-and-use-tax/out-of-state-dealers-marketplace-facilitators.html) and [SUT-4 - Nexus Overview](https://revenue.support.tn.gov/hc/en-us/articles/360058589291-SUT-4-Nexus-Overview)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Tennessee Taxpayer Access Point (TNTAP) under "Register a New Business."
- **Official registration portal:** [TNTAP - Tennessee Taxpayer Access Point](https://tntap.tn.gov/eservices/_/)
- **Registration fees:** No specific registration fee is mentioned in official sources for sales tax registration.
- **Registration info page:** [Registration - TN DOR](https://www.tn.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-and-use-tax/registration.html) and [SUT-10 - Registering for an Account](https://revenue.support.tn.gov/hc/en-us/articles/360058139252-SUT-10-Sales-and-Use-Tax-Account-Registering-for-an-Account)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Tennessee taxes most tangible personal property at the 7% state rate.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Taxable, but at a reduced state rate of 4% (plus applicable local tax). Prepared food, candy, dietary supplements, and alcoholic beverages are taxed at the full 7% rate.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 7% state rate (no general clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax.
  - **Other exemptions:** Textbooks, school meals, certain healthcare products, prosthetic devices, and durable medical equipment (with prescription).
- **Official exemption information:** [Exemptions, Certificates & Credits](https://www.tn.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-and-use-tax/exemptions-certificates-credits.html) and [Other Exemptions](https://www.tn.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-and-use-tax/exemptions-certificates-credits/other-exemptions.html)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Local jurisdictions impose additional sales tax.
- **Range of local rates:** Up to 2.75% (must be in multiples of 0.25%)
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Out-of-state dealers meeting the nexus threshold must collect both state and applicable local taxes.
- **Local rates map:** [Local Sales Tax Rates Map](https://www.tn.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-and-use-tax/local-sales-tax/local-sales-tax-rates-map.html)

Sources:

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Accounts are automatically set up for monthly filing. Businesses with an average tax liability of $1,000 or less per month over a 12-month period may elect to file quarterly. Annual filing is also available for qualifying businesses.
- **Filing deadlines:** The 20th day of the month following the end of the reporting period (monthly, quarterly, or annual).
- **Official filing portal:** [TNTAP - Tennessee Taxpayer Access Point](https://tntap.tn.gov/eservices/_/)
- **All returns and payments must be submitted electronically** through TNTAP.
