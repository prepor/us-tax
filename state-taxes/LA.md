# Louisiana (LA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 5% (effective January 1, 2025, increased from 4.45%; scheduled to decrease to 4.75% on January 1, 2030)
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** ~10% on average (ranges from ~7% to as high as 13.5% in some municipalities)

Sources: [What is the state sales tax rate? (LA DOR)](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-education-and-faqs/faqs/sales-tax-reform/what-is-the-state-sales-tax-rate/) | [General Sales & Use Tax](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/businesses/sales-taxes/general-sales-use-tax/)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales of tangible personal property or services delivered into Louisiana
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective August 1, 2023, via HB 171)
- **Measurement period:** Previous or current calendar year
- **When the law took effect:** Louisiana adopted economic nexus requirements following the 2018 Wayfair decision. Remote sellers meeting the revenue threshold are required to register and collect both state and local sales tax.
- **Official guidance:** [Remote Sellers FAQ (PDF)](https://dam.ldr.la.gov/miscellaneous/Remote%20Sellers%20FAQs.pdf) | [Do businesses with Internet sales have to collect Louisiana sales tax?](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-education-and-faqs/faqs/sales-tax/do-businesses-with-internet-sales-have-to-collect-louisiana-sales-tax-on-their-sales/) | [Remote Sellers Information Bulletins](https://www.revenue.louisiana.gov/LawsAndPolicies/RemoteSellersInformationBulletins)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Remote sellers without physical presence should register with the **Louisiana Sales and Use Tax Commission for Remote Sellers** to file a single return covering both state and local taxes. Registration is done through LaTAP (Louisiana Taxpayer Access Portal).
- **Registration portal:** [LaTAP](https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/_/) | [Louisiana Sales and Use Tax Commission for Remote Sellers](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-policy/general-resources/louisiana-sales-and-use-tax-commission-for-remote-sellers/)
- **Registration fee:** No fee noted for remote seller registration
- **Alternative:** Businesses may also voluntarily register as a "Direct Marketer" to collect and remit a combined state and local rate of 9% on all taxable purchases (rate increased from 8.45% to 9% effective January 1, 2025).

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property at retail are subject to Louisiana sales and use tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food for home consumption:** Exempt. Food sold for preparation and consumption in the home (grocery staples including bakery products) is exempt from state sales tax. Prepared food is taxable.
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from state sales tax
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full rate (no general exemption)
  - **Residential utilities:** Exempt from state sales tax
- **2025 reform note:** Several exemptions were repealed and some reinstated as part of the 2024-2025 tax reform. Consult the current list.
- **Official information:** [Are there any exemptions from the sales tax?](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-education-and-faqs/faqs/sales-tax/are-there-any-exemptions-from-the-sales-tax/) | [Exemptions retained after Jan 1, 2025](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-education-and-faqs/faqs/sales-tax-reform/which-existing-sales-tax-exemptions-were-retained-for-taxable-periods-beginning-on-january-1-2025/) | [Exemptions repealed as of Jan 1, 2025](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/tax-education-and-faqs/faqs/sales-tax-reform/which-sales-and-use-tax-exemptions-are-being-repealed/)

## Local Taxes
- **Can parishes/municipalities add local sales tax?** Yes. Louisiana has 64 parishes, each of which sets its own local sales tax rate.
- **Range of local rates:** Approximately 2% to 7% (parish-level); combined state + local rates range from ~7% to ~13.5%
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers who register with the Louisiana Sales and Use Tax Commission for Remote Sellers file a single return covering both state and local taxes.
- **Parish rate lookup:** [Parish & Municipality Tax Table](https://taxes.mfn.dps.la.gov/) | [Parish E-File Lookup](https://parishe-file.revenue.louisiana.gov/lookup/lookup.aspx)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly (default). Businesses that seldom have taxable transactions may qualify for quarterly filing.
- **Filing deadline:** Returns and payments are due on or before the **20th of the month** following the close of the reporting period
- **Filing portals:**
  - **State tax:** [LaTAP (Louisiana Taxpayer Access Portal)](https://latap.revenue.louisiana.gov/_/)
  - **Parish/local tax:** [Parish E-File](https://parishe-file.revenue.louisiana.gov/)
  - **Remote sellers:** File through the Commission for Remote Sellers (single combined return)
- **Official filing info:** [Sales Tax Filing Options](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/businesses/sales-taxes/sales-tax-filing-options/) | [Filing Dates Calendar](https://revenue.louisiana.gov/calendar/)
