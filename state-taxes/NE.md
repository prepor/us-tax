# Nebraska (NE) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does Nebraska have a sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 5.5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies by location; state rate of 5.5% plus local rates of 0% to 2%, resulting in combined rates ranging from approximately 5.5% to 7.5%

Source: [Nebraska Sales and Use Tax](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/nebraska-sales-and-use-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in Nebraska retail sales in the prior or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective January 1, 2024, via LB 388; only the $100,000 revenue threshold applies)
- **When the law took effect:** April 1, 2019 (LB 284); transaction threshold repealed January 1, 2024 (LB 388)
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Seller and Marketplace Facilitator FAQs](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/about/frequently-asked-questions/remote-seller-and-marketplace-facilitator-faqs)

Remote sellers must obtain a sales tax permit and begin collecting and remitting sales tax on or before the first day of the second calendar month after the threshold is exceeded.

Sources:

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Complete the Nebraska Tax Application (Form 20) online or by mail. The online application is available for businesses that do not already have a Nebraska Tax ID Number.
- **Official registration portal:** [Register Your New Business Online](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/register-your-new-business-online)
- **Form 20 (paper application):** [Nebraska Tax Application, Form 20](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/sites/default/files/doc/tax-forms/f_20.pdf)
- **Registration fees:** No fee is mentioned for standard sales tax permit registration on the official website. (A $10 nonrefundable fee applies only for Direct Payment Permit authorization.)
- Once issued, the sales tax permit does not require renewal.

Source: [Starting a Business in Nebraska](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/starting-business-nebraska)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Most tangible personal property sold at retail is subject to Nebraska sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food and food ingredients:** Exempt from sales tax (unprepared grocery food). However, prepared food (sold heated, with utensils, or as mixed ingredients) is taxable. Food sold through vending machines is taxable.
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable (Nebraska does not exempt clothing from sales tax).
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from sales tax.

Sources:

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Cities can impose local sales tax under the Local Option Revenue Act. Counties can impose sales tax under Neb. Rev. Stat. sections 13-319 to 13-326 (currently only Dakota and Gage counties impose a county sales tax).
- **Range of local rates:** 0.5%, 1%, 1.5%, 1.75%, or 2%
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers that meet the economic nexus thresholds must collect both Nebraska state and applicable local sales taxes based on the delivery address.
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Nebraska Sales Tax Rate Finder](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/sales-and-use-tax/sales-tax-rate-finder)

Sources:

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by annual tax liability:
  - **Monthly:** If yearly tax liability is $3,000 or more
  - **Quarterly:** If yearly tax liability is $900 or more but less than $3,000
  - **Annually:** If yearly tax liability is less than $900
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns are due on or before the 20th day of the month following the reporting period.
- **Filing portal:** [Nebraska Sales and Use Tax Online Filing](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/nebraska-sales-and-use-tax-online-filing)
- **Online filing system:** [Online Filing for Nebraska Business Taxes](https://revenue.nebraska.gov/businesses/online-filing-nebraska-business-taxes)
