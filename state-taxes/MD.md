# Maryland (MD) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6% (9% on alcoholic beverages)
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6% (Maryland has no local sales taxes; the rate is uniform statewide)

Source: [Maryland Sales & Use Tax](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/business/sales-use/) | [6% Rate Chart (PDF)](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/forms/Tax_rate_chart.pdf)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales of tangible personal property or taxable services delivered in Maryland
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate transactions for delivery into Maryland
- **Measurement period:** Previous or current calendar year (either threshold triggers nexus)
- **Effective date:** October 1, 2018 (implemented following the Wayfair Supreme Court decision)
- **Note:** All sales, including tax-exempt transactions and tax-exempt revenue, count toward the threshold calculation.
- **Official guidance:** [Sales and Use Tax for Out-of-State Vendors](https://services.marylandcomptroller.gov/taxes/en/sales-and-use-tax-for-out-of-state-vendors?id=kb_article_view&sysparm_article=KB0010169) | [Sales and Use Tax Alert - September 2018 (PDF)](https://www.marylandcomptroller.gov/content/dam/mdcomp/tax/legal-publications/alerts/SUT-Tax-Alert-Sept2018.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online using the Maryland Combined Registration Application (CRA) through Maryland Tax Connect or Maryland Business Express.
- **Registration portals:**
  - [Maryland Tax Connect (mdtaxconnect.gov)](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/mdtaxconnect/)
  - [Combined Registration Application](https://interactive.marylandtaxes.gov/webapps/comptrollercra/entrance.asp)
  - [Maryland Business Express](https://businessexpress.maryland.gov/start/taxes-and-insurance)
- **Registration fee:** No fee noted for sales and use tax registration
- **Additional help:** Call 1-800-MDTAXES (1-800-638-2937) or schedule an appointment at a local Comptroller's office

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Maryland imposes a 6% sales and use tax on retail sales of tangible personal property and certain services.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food and groceries:** Exempt when sold by a substantial grocery or market business for consumption off-premises. However, **soft drinks, bottled water, alcoholic beverages, candy, and confectionery** are taxable (not considered "food" for exemption purposes). Prepared food is taxable.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 6% rate (no exemption)
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from sales tax
- **Data/IT services tax (effective July 1, 2025):** A 3% sales and use tax applies to data and information technology services under HB 352 (Budget Reconciliation and Financing Act of 2025). Covered services include SaaS, cloud hosting (IaaS/PaaS), custom software development, data processing, and IT consulting (NAICS codes 518, 519, 5415, 5132). SaaS sold for enterprise/commercial use is taxed at 3%. Note: SaaS sold to individuals for personal use was already subject to the standard 6% sales tax as tangible personal property (preexisting law, not introduced by HB 352).
- **Official exemption list:** [Sales Tax Exemptions](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/business/sales-use/tax-exemptions/) | [Sales of Food Guide](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/business/sales-use/special-situations/sales-of-food.php) | [List of Tangible Personal Property and Services (PDF)](https://marylandtaxes.gov/forms/Tax_Publications/Sales_and_Use_Tax-List_of_TPP_and_Services.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. Maryland does not have local sales and use taxes. The 6% state rate applies uniformly across the entire state.
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Not applicable

Source: [Sales and Use Tax FAQ](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/faq/sales-use.php)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Initially quarterly. Based on actual payment amounts, the Comptroller may change your schedule to monthly, quarterly, bi-annual, or annual filing. You will be notified in advance of any change.
- **Filing deadline:** Returns are due on or before the **20th of the month** following the end of the reporting period. If the 20th falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, the return is due the next business day.
- **Filing portals:**
  - [Maryland Tax Connect](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/mdtaxconnect/)
  - [bFile](https://interactive.marylandtaxes.gov/business/bfile/osc/selectapp.aspx)
- **Due dates reference:** [Sales and Use Tax Due Dates](https://taxes.marylandtaxes.gov/Business_Taxes/General_Information/Filing_Deadlines/Sales_and_Use_Due_Dates.shtml) | [Deadlines and Due Dates](https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/pros/deadlines-and-duedates.php)
