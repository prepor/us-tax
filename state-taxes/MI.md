# Michigan (MI) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6% (Michigan does not allow local sales taxes)

Source: [Sales and Use Taxes | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales into Michigan in the previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more transactions into Michigan in the previous calendar year
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2018 (codified into law via 2019 PAs 145 and 146)
- **Note:** The threshold calculation includes both taxable and non-taxable sales and transactions. Obligation to collect begins January 1 of the calendar year following the year the threshold was exceeded.
- **Link to official nexus information:** [Sales and Use Tax Information for Remote Sellers | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/resources/sales-and-use-tax-information-for-remote-sellers)

Additional reference: [Remote Seller FAQ | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/information/remote-seller-faq)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through Michigan Treasury Online (MTO) using the eRegistration process, or by mail using Form 518 (Registration for Michigan Taxes). Online registration is faster and requires an FEIN.
- **Official registration portal:** [Michigan Treasury Online (MTO)](https://mto.treasury.michigan.gov/)
- **Registration by mail:** Michigan Department of Treasury, PO Box 30778, Lansing, MI 48909
- **Registration fees:** No fee mentioned; a sales tax license is issued upon registration.
- **License copy requests:** Call the Registration Unit at 517-636-6925

Source: [New Business Registration | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/new-biz)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to the 6% sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Grocery-type food for human consumption is exempt (excluding tobacco, marijuana, and alcoholic beverages). Prepared food (sold in a heated state, mixed by the seller, or sold with eating utensils) is taxable. **Note:** Unlike most states, Michigan treats candy and soft drinks/soda as exempt groceries -- they are not taxed. This is important for sellers of those product categories.
  - **Clothing:** Not exempt; clothing is generally taxable in Michigan.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt per the Michigan Constitution (effective January 1, 1975). Over-the-counter drugs legally dispensed by prescription are also exempt.
  - **Medical equipment:** Hearing aids, hearing aid batteries, prescribed eyeglasses and contact lenses, and devices used to replace body parts or assist disabled persons (with a prescription) are exempt.
- **Link to official exemption information:** [Exemptions FAQ | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/information/exemptions-faq)

Additional reference: [Common Sales and Use Tax Exemptions and Requirements | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/common-sales-and-use-tax-exemptions-and-requirements)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. Michigan does not allow city, local, or county sales taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** N/A
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** N/A

Source: [Sales and Use Taxes | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned upon registration based on estimated level of activity; reviewed annually based on prior year liability.
  - **Monthly:** Higher-liability taxpayers
  - **Quarterly:** Mid-range liability taxpayers
  - **Annual:** Lower-liability taxpayers
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - Monthly/quarterly returns: Due by the 20th of the month following the return period
  - Annual return (Form 5081): Due by February 28 of the following year (required for all filers regardless of frequency)
- **Note:** You must file a return within your established filing frequency even if no tax is due.
- **Filing portal:** [Michigan Treasury Online (MTO)](https://mto.treasury.michigan.gov/)

Source: [Filing Requirements FAQ | Michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/information/filing-requirements-faq)
