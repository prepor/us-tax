# North Dakota (ND) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 5.0% ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 6.06%. Combined rates range up to 8.5% depending on local jurisdiction ([Local Taxes - City and County Taxes](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax/local-taxes-city-and-county-taxes))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in taxable sales into North Dakota in the current or previous calendar year ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was repealed effective July 1, 2019, under S.B. 2191) ([Sovos - North Dakota Economic Nexus](https://sovos.com/blog/sut/did-north-dakotas-economic-nexus-change/))
- **Effective date:** October 1, 2018 (original law); transaction threshold removed July 1, 2019 ([Sales Tax Institute - ND Economic Nexus](https://www.salestaxinstitute.com/resources/north-dakota-enacts-economic-nexus-legislation-pending-quill-decision))
- **Registration timing:** Remote sellers have 60 days after first meeting the $100,000 threshold to register and begin collecting tax ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Official nexus guidance:** [Sales and Use Tax Requirements (PDF)](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sites/www/files/documents/guidelines/business/sales-use/sales-use-and-gross-receipts-tax-requirements-1.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Two options:
  1. **ND TAP (Taxpayer Access Point):** Complete the online application on ND TAP for North Dakota-only registration ([Instructions to Sign Up for ND TAP](https://www.tax.nd.gov/instructions-sign-nd-tap))
  2. **Streamlined Sales Tax Registration:** Register with multiple SST member states through one application at [www.sstregister.org](https://www.sstregister.org) ([Streamlined Sales Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Registration portal:** [ND TAP - North Dakota Taxpayer Access Point](https://tap.tax.nd.gov/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register (*Note: official source not explicitly found; standard among SST states*)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally taxable ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/groceries:** Food and food ingredients for home consumption are exempt. Exceptions: candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, and prepared food are taxable ([Sales Tax Exemptions - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sales-tax-exemptions))
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable; no clothing exemption in North Dakota ([Sales Tax Exemptions - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sales-tax-exemptions))
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax. Over-the-counter medicines are taxable ([Guideline - Exemptions (PDF)](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sites/www/files/documents/guidelines/business/sales-use/guideline-exemptions.pdf))
- **Official exemption list:** [Sales Tax Exemptions - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sales-tax-exemptions)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Cities and counties may impose local option sales taxes ([Local Taxes - City and County Taxes](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax/local-taxes-city-and-county-taxes))
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 3.5% (maximum allowed by law is 3.5%), resulting in combined rates of 5% to 8.5% ([Local Taxes by Location (PDF)](https://www.tax.nd.gov/sites/www/files/documents/guidelines/business/sales-use/local-taxes-by-location.pdf))
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect both state and applicable local option taxes based on the delivery destination ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Local rate changes:** Rates may change periodically; see [City and County Local Tax Rate Changes](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax/local-taxes-city-and-county-taxes/city-and-county-local-tax-rate-changes) for updates

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually, as assigned when you apply for your permit based on sales volume ([Sales and Use Tax - ND Tax Commissioner](https://www.tax.nd.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns must be filed each assigned period whether or not tax is due. See the official deadline schedule for specific due dates ([Sales and Use Tax Deadlines](https://www.tax.nd.gov/news/resources/tax-deadlines/sales-and-use-tax-deadlines))
- **Filing portal:** [ND TAP - North Dakota Taxpayer Access Point](https://tap.tax.nd.gov/)
