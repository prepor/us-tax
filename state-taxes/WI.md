# Wisconsin (WI) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Ranges from 5% to 7.9% depending on locality. Seventy counties impose a 0.5% county tax (Milwaukee County imposes 0.9% effective January 1, 2024), and the City of Milwaukee imposes a 2% city tax (effective January 1, 2024).

Source: [DOR Sales and Use Tax | Wisconsin Department of Revenue](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/Pages/SalesAndUse/Home.aspx)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales into Wisconsin in the previous or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective February 20, 2021)
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2018 (original); amended February 20, 2021 (removed transaction threshold)
- **Calendar year measurement:** Remote sellers must use a calendar year to determine economic nexus
- **Link to official nexus information:** [DOR Remote Sellers Common Questions | WI DOR](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/Pages/FAQS/ise-remote-sellers.aspx)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Complete the online Business Tax Registration through My Tax Account
- **Registration portal:** [My Tax Account | WI DOR](https://tap.revenue.wi.gov/mta/_/)
- **Registration fees:** $20 initial registration fee; $10 renewal fee
- **Processing time:** Typically one business day after the department receives the application
- **Business Tax Registration info:** [Business Tax Registration | WI DOR](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/Pages/FAQS/pcs-btr.aspx)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Groceries / food for home consumption:** Exempt, but "candy," "soft drinks," and "prepared food" are taxable
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full rate (no general clothing exemption)
  - **Over-the-counter drugs:** Exempt
  - **Medical devices and equipment (with prescription):** Exempt
- **Link to official exemptions:** [DOR Sales and Use Tax Exemptions | WI DOR](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/Pages/FAQS/pcs-s-exempt.aspx)
- **Exemption certificate instructions:** [Instructions for Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Exemption Certificate (S-211)](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/DORForms/s-211-inst.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties/cities add local sales tax?** Yes
  - **County tax:** 70 counties impose a 0.5% county sales and use tax; Milwaukee County imposes a 0.9% county tax (increased from 0.5% effective January 1, 2024)
  - **City of Milwaukee:** 2% city sales and use tax (effective January 1, 2024)
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 2.9% (0.9% Milwaukee County + 2% City of Milwaukee where applicable; 0.5% county elsewhere)
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes, remote sellers must collect applicable county and city taxes based on the destination of the sale
- **County tax information:** [Sales and Use Tax for Wisconsin Counties and Municipalities (Pub 209)](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/DOR%20Publications/pb209.pdf)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned upon registration:
  - Monthly
  - Quarterly
  - Annually (calendar-year annual return due January 31)
- **Filing deadlines:** Due the last day of the month following the reporting period. Annual returns for fiscal-year filers are due the last day of the month following the fiscal year end. If a due date falls on a weekend or legal holiday, the due date extends to the next business day.
- **Electronic filing:** Mandatory for all sales and use tax registrants. A waiver may be requested for undue hardship.
- **Filing portal:** [My Tax Account | WI DOR](https://tap.revenue.wi.gov/mta/_/)
- **Electronic filing options:** [DOR Sales and Use Tax Electronic Filing Options | WI DOR](https://www.revenue.wi.gov/Pages/FAQS/pcs-e-option.aspx)
- **A return must be filed for each reporting period even if no tax is due**
