# Mississippi (MS) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 7%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 7% for most transactions (Mississippi has very limited local sales taxes; Jackson levies an additional 1% infrastructure tax)

Source: [Sales Tax Rates | Mississippi Department of Revenue](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/sales-tax-rates)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $250,000 in sales into Mississippi over any 12-month period
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Mississippi uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2018
- **Link to official nexus information:** [Business Tax Frequently Asked Questions | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/business-tax-frequently-asked-questions)

Additional reference: [Mississippi Sales and Use Taxes | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/mississippi-sales-and-use-taxes)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through TAP (Taxpayer Access Point). A packet containing your permit and filing information will be mailed to you; allow two weeks to receive it. A separate permit is required for each business location. Permits do not expire and do not require renewal as long as you continue in the same business at the same location.
- **Official registration portal:** [Register for Taxes | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/register-taxes) / [TAP Portal](https://tap.dor.ms.gov/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee mentioned in official resources.

Source: [Registration Information for Sales and Use Tax Applicants | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/registration-information-sales-and-use-tax-applicants)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. All sales of tangible personal property in Mississippi are subject to the regular retail rate of 7% unless otherwise exempted or subject to a reduced rate.
- **Notable exemptions and reduced rates:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Taxed at a reduced rate of 5% (applies to food and drink for human consumption eligible to be purchased with SNAP benefits). Food purchased with food stamps is fully exempt.
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable at the full 7% rate. An annual Sales Tax Holiday (last Friday in July through the following Saturday) exempts clothing and footwear under $100 per item.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt. Prescription drugs that may only be legally dispensed by a licensed pharmacist upon written authority from a licensed practitioner are exempt.
- **Link to official exemption information:** [Sales Tax Exemptions | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/sales-tax-exemptions)

Additional reference: [Reduced Sales Tax on Groceries Begins July 1 | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/news/reduced-sales-tax-groceries-begins-july-1)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Very limited. Mississippi has minimal local sales tax authority:
  - **Jackson Infrastructure Tax:** The City of Jackson levies an additional 1% on certain retail sales within the corporate limits.
  - **Tupelo:** The City of Tupelo levies an additional 0.25% local sales tax.
  - **Tourism and Economic Development Taxes:** Various cities and counties impose additional taxes on hotels, motels, restaurants, and bars -- these generally do not apply to typical retail sales of physical goods.
  - Counties do not levy general local sales taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 1% (Jackson has a 1% local sales tax; Tupelo has a 0.25% local sales tax)
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** Generally not applicable for most locations. If selling to customers in Jackson, the additional 1% infrastructure tax may apply. Contact the Mississippi DOR for specific guidance.

Source: [Sales Tax Rates | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/sales-tax-rates)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** Required if annual total payment is $3,600 or more (average liability of $300+ per month)
  - **Quarterly:** Permitted if annual total payment is less than $3,600
  - Filing frequencies are reviewed and adjusted annually by the Department of Revenue.
- **Filing deadlines:** Tax is due by the 20th day of the month in which the tax is due. A discount is allowed if payment is made by this date.
- **Online filing:** Required on TAP if you have more than one sales tax account/location, report more than 4 rates of tax, or are subject to certain other taxes/fees. Online filing is free.
- **Filing portal:** [TAP (Taxpayer Access Point)](https://tap.dor.ms.gov/) / [Online Filing | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/online-filing)

Source: [Reporting Requirements | Mississippi DOR](https://www.dor.ms.gov/business/sales-use-tax/reporting-requirements)
