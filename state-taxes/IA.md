# Iowa (IA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does the state have sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.0% ([Iowa Tax/Fee Descriptions and Rates - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/iowa-tax-fee-descriptions-and-rates))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Up to 7.0% in jurisdictions with a 1% local option sales tax (LOST). Many cities and counties impose the additional 1% LOST. ([Permits, Filing Requirements, & Local Option Sales Tax (LOST) - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/permit-requirements))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 or more in gross revenue from sales into Iowa (includes exempt sales, wholesale sales, sales for resale, and sales for which a marketplace facilitator collected sales tax)
- **Transaction threshold:** None. Iowa uses only a revenue-based threshold.
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2019 (enacted via Senate File 2417, signed May 30, 2018) ([Remote Sellers and Iowa Tax - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/remote-sellers))
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers and Iowa Tax - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/remote-sellers)
- **Important note:** If you exceed the $100,000 threshold during the current year, you must begin collecting Iowa sales tax on the first day of the next calendar month that starts at least 30 days after exceeding the threshold.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register for an Iowa sales tax permit through GovConnectIowa, the state's online tax portal. Alternatively, you can register through the Streamlined Sales Tax Registration System (SSTRS) if you want to register in multiple member states at once. ([Business Permit Registration - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/businesses/business-permit-registration))
- **Online registration portal:** [GovConnectIowa](https://govconnect.iowa.gov/) via [Iowa DOR Business Permit Registration](https://revenue.iowa.gov/businesses/business-permit-registration)
- **Streamlined Sales Tax registration:** [SSTRS Registration](https://www.sstregister.org/) - Iowa is a member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement ([Streamlined Sales Tax - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/streamlined-sales-tax-filing-and-payment-information))
- **Registration fee:** No fee to register for a sales tax permit

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to Iowa sales tax. ([Iowa Sales and Use Tax Guide - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/iowa-sales-and-use-tax-guide))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Exempt. Food and food ingredients for home consumption are exempt from Iowa sales tax. This does NOT include candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, prepared food, alcoholic beverages, or tobacco. ([Iowa Sales Tax on Food - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/iowa-sales-tax-food))
  - **Clothing:** Taxable. Regular clothing purchases are subject to sales tax. Iowa holds an annual sales tax holiday (first Friday and Saturday of August) during which clothing and footwear priced under $100 per item are exempt. ([Iowa's Annual Sales Tax Holiday - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/iowas-annual-sales-tax-holiday))
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt. Prescription drugs dispensed to the ultimate user are exempt from sales tax. Over-the-counter medications (aspirin, cold remedies, etc.) are taxable unless purchased with a prescription. ([Medical Clinics and Related Businesses - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/medical-clinics-and-related-businesses-iowa-sales-and-use-tax-information))
- **Official guide:** [Iowa Sales and Use Tax Guide - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/iowa-sales-and-use-tax-guide)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Many Iowa jurisdictions impose a 1% Local Option Sales Tax (LOST) in addition to the 6% state rate. ([Permits, Filing Requirements, & Local Option Sales Tax (LOST) - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/permit-requirements))
- **Local tax rate:** 1% where applicable (voted on by local jurisdiction)
- **Remote seller collection:** Yes, remote sellers must collect applicable local option sales tax in addition to the state rate. The LOST is destination-based, meaning you collect it if the buyer is in a jurisdiction that has adopted it. ([Remote Sellers and Iowa Tax - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/remote-sellers))
- **Current LOST jurisdictions:** A list of jurisdictions with LOST is available on the Iowa DOR website, updated in June and December. ([Permits, Filing Requirements, & LOST - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/permit-requirements))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined automatically based on information provided during registration:
  - **Monthly:** Taxpayers collecting more than $1,200 in sales/use tax per year file monthly
  - **Annual:** Taxpayers collecting less than $1,200 in sales/use tax per year file annually
  - ([Filing Frequency & Return Due Dates - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/filing-frequencies-and-return-due-dates))
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - **Monthly:** Due on the last day of the month following the reporting period (e.g., January's return is due February 28/29)
  - **Annual:** Due January 31 of the following year
  - ([Sales and Use Tax Permit, Return Filing, and Payment Changes - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/sales-and-use-tax-permit-return-filing-and-payment-changes))
- **Filing portal:** [GovConnectIowa](https://govconnect.iowa.gov/) ([GovConnectIowa Help - Iowa DOR](https://revenue.iowa.gov/govconnectiowa))
- **Note:** Monthly filers file a single combined return for all sales and use tax collected each month.
