# Alabama (AL) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4% (general rate); 2% reduced rate on food effective September 1, 2025
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** ~9.44%

Sources:
- State rates: [State Sales and Use Tax Rates - Alabama Department of Revenue](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/sales-use/state-sales-use-tax-rates/)
- Food rate reduction: [NOTICE State Sales and Use Tax Rate Reduced on Food Beginning September 1, 2025](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/notice-state-sales-and-use-tax-rate-reduced-on-food-beginning-september-1-2025/)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $250,000 in total retail sales delivered into Alabama in the previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **Effective date:** October 1, 2018 (Rule 810-6-2-.90.03 enforced prospectively from this date)
- **When obligation begins:** The nexus obligation starts January 1 of the year following the year in which the $250,000 threshold was exceeded.
- **Official nexus rule:** [Rule 810-6-2-.90.03 (PDF)](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/810-6-2-.90.03.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Remote sellers can register for the Simplified Sellers Use Tax (SSUT) program through the My Alabama Taxes (MAT) portal. Click "Obtain a new tax account" to apply for an SSUT account online.
- **Registration portal:** [My Alabama Taxes (MAT)](https://myalabamataxes.alabama.gov)
- **Additional registration info:** [Business Tax Online Registration System](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/sales-use/business-tax-online-registration-system/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee specified for SSUT registration

Sources:

## Taxable Goods
- **Physical goods generally taxable:** Yes
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - Prescription drugs
  - Gasoline and motor oil (subject to separate excise taxes)
  - Fertilizer, insecticides, and fungicides used for agricultural purposes
  - Seeds for planting purposes
  - Feed for livestock and poultry
- **Food/groceries:** Taxable, but at a reduced state rate of 2% (as of September 1, 2025). Local taxes may still apply at full rate.
- **Clothing:** Fully taxable at the standard rate (no exemption)
- **Medicine:** Prescription drugs are exempt; over-the-counter medicines are taxable

**SSUT Note:** Remote sellers participating in the SSUT program collect a flat 8% rate on all sales into Alabama, which covers both state and local obligations.

## Local Taxes
- **Cities/counties can add local sales tax:** Yes. Alabama has one of the most complex local tax systems in the country.
- **Range of local rates:** Local rates typically range from 1% to 7.5%, resulting in combined rates from roughly 5% to 12.5%
- **Remote seller local tax obligations:** Remote sellers using the SSUT program collect a single flat 8% rate, which is distributed to state and local jurisdictions. This relieves the seller from tracking individual local rates.

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Default is monthly. You may request:
  - **Quarterly** if tax liability is less than $2,400/year
  - **Semi-annual** if tax liability is less than $1,200/year
  - **Annual** if tax liability is less than $600/year or sales occur in no more than one 30-day period
- **Filing deadlines:** Due on or before the 20th of the month following the reporting period. SSUT returns follow the same schedule (due by the 20th of each month).
- **Filing portal:** [My Alabama Taxes (MAT)](https://myalabamataxes.alabama.gov) -- electronic filing is required
- **Discount:** SSUT participants may deduct and retain 2% of the tax properly collected and remitted on time
