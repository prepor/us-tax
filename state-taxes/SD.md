# South Dakota (SD) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.2% (effective July 1, 2023)
- **Sunset clause:** The 4.2% rate is temporary under HB 1137 and is scheduled to revert to 4.5% on July 1, 2027 unless the legislature acts to make it permanent.
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 6.0%--6.4%, depending on municipality. Municipal sales tax rates range from 1% to 2%, and some municipalities also impose a 1% municipal gross receipts tax (MGRT).

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales into South Dakota in the previous or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective July 1, 2023)
- **When the law took effect:** South Dakota's original remote seller law (SB 106) was enacted in 2016 and was the subject of the landmark U.S. Supreme Court decision *South Dakota v. Wayfair, Inc.* on June 21, 2018, which upheld the law. The current $100,000-only threshold took effect July 1, 2023.
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - Are You Collecting Sales Tax?](https://dor.sd.gov/newsroom/remote-sellers-are-you-collecting-sales-tax/) and [Remote Seller Bulletin (PDF)](https://dor.sd.gov/media/yh0n3oc2/remote-seller-bulletin.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply online using the South Dakota Tax License Application at [sd.gov/taxapp](https://apps.sd.gov/rv23cedar/main/main.aspx). You can also set up an EPath account during registration for electronic filing.
- **Official registration portal:** [Tax License Application](https://apps.sd.gov/rv23cedar/main/main.aspx)
- **Registration fees:** No specific registration fee is noted in official sources. Contact the Department of Revenue at 1-800-829-9188 to confirm.

Sources:
- [Online Services - SD DOR](https://dor.sd.gov/online-services/)
- [EPath](https://apps.sd.gov/rv23epath/login.aspx)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. South Dakota taxes most tangible personal property.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Generally taxable at the full state rate. SNAP (food stamp) purchases are exempt. Purchases under the Child Nutrition program (WIC) are also exempt.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable (no general clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Insulin for human use is exempt whether sold with or without a prescription. Durable medical equipment with a prescription is exempt.
  - **Other exemptions:** Sales to certain exempt entities (relief agencies, religious and private schools) that have been approved by the Department.
- **Official exemption information:** [Health Services, Drugs, and Medical Devices (PDF)](https://dor.sd.gov/media/skpmuvgy/health-services.pdf) and [Exempt Entities (PDF)](https://dor.sd.gov/media/sgxnhnhl/exemptentities.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Municipalities may impose a general municipal sales tax of up to 2%, plus an additional 1% municipal gross receipts tax (MGRT).
- **Range of local rates:** 1% to 2% municipal sales tax (plus potential 1% MGRT)
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers who meet the economic nexus threshold must collect applicable municipal taxes in addition to the state tax.
- **Rate lookup tool:** [SD Sales Tax Rate Lookup](https://apps.sd.gov/rv25taxmatch/main.aspx)

Sources:
- [Municipal Tax - SD DOR](https://dor.sd.gov/businesses/taxes/municipal-tax/)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by the Department upon registration. Filing may be monthly, quarterly, or annually based on tax liability.
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns are due by the 20th of the month following the reporting period (e.g., January sales are due by February 20th), regardless of filing frequency.
- **Official filing portal:** [EPath - Online Filing & Payment](https://apps.sd.gov/rv23epath/login.aspx)
- **Filing help:** [Filing and Paying Taxes Online Help](https://dor.sd.gov/businesses/taxes/sales-use-tax/filing-and-paying-taxes-online-help/)
