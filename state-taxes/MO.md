# Missouri (MO) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.225%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies significantly by location. Cities, counties, and special taxing districts may impose additional sales taxes. Use the Missouri Sales and Use Tax Lookup tool for specific rates.

Source: [Sales/Use Tax | Missouri Department of Revenue](https://dor.mo.gov/taxation/business/tax-types/sales-use/)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross receipts from taxable sales of tangible personal property into Missouri in a calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Missouri uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** January 1, 2023
- **Note:** At the end of each calendar quarter, determine if your gross receipts from taxable sales in Missouri exceed $100,000 over the preceding 12-month period. If exceeded, you must begin collecting and remitting vendor's use tax no later than three months following the close of that quarter. The $100,000 threshold includes sales through a marketplace facilitator.
- **Link to official nexus information:** [FAQs - Remote Seller and Marketplace Facilitator | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/faq/taxation/business/remote-seller-and-marketplace-facilitator.html)

Additional reference: [RSMo Section 144.752](https://revisor.mo.gov/main/OneSection.aspx?section=144.752)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Missouri Tax Registration system or by submitting Form 2643 (Missouri Tax Registration Application). After registration, you will receive a welcome letter with your Missouri Tax Identification Number (MOID), a 4-digit PIN for online filing, and your assigned filing frequency.
- **Official registration portal:** [Register Business | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/register-business/) / [MyTax Missouri](https://mytax.mo.gov/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee mentioned in official resources.
- **Contact:** Business Tax Registration team at 573-751-5860 or businesstaxregister@dor.mo.gov

Source: [Business Tax Registration | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/taxation/business/registration/requirements.html)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to sales/use tax.
- **Notable exemptions and reduced rates:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Taxed at a reduced state rate of 1.225% (a 3% reduction from the regular 4.225% rate). This applies to food items eligible for purchase with food stamps/SNAP benefits, including food for home consumption, seeds, and plants for personal food gardens. Note: all local sales taxes still apply to food in full.
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable at the full rate. No general exemption for clothing in Missouri.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt. Prescription drugs dispensed by a licensed pharmacist upon written authority from a licensed practitioner are exempt.
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Taxable (unless prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider).
  - **Essential health and hygiene products:** Diapers (child and adult), feminine hygiene products (tampons, pads, liners, cups, menstrual discs, period underwear), and incontinence products are exempt from both state and local sales/use taxes effective August 28, 2025 (HB 508).
- **Link to official exemption information:** [Sales and Use Tax Exemptions and Exclusions | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/taxation/business/tax-types/sales-use/exemptions.php)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Missouri has extensive local sales taxes. Cities, counties, and special taxing districts (e.g., fire districts, transportation districts) can impose additional sales taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** Local rates vary widely; combined state + local rates can range from 4.225% (state only) up to approximately 11%+ in some areas.
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** It depends. If a political subdivision has passed a local use tax, remote sellers must collect it. If a political subdivision has **not** passed a local use tax, remote sellers only collect the state use tax portion of 4.225%.
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Sales/Use Tax Rate Tables | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/taxation/business/tax-types/sales-use/rate-tables/) and the [2026 Statewide Sales/Use Tax Rate Tables](https://dor.mo.gov/taxation/business/tax-types/sales-use/rate-tables/2026/)

Source: [FAQs - Remote Seller and Marketplace Facilitator | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/faq/taxation/business/remote-seller-and-marketplace-facilitator.html)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by the amount of state tax due:
  - **Monthly:** If state taxes collected are $500 or more per month
  - **Quarterly:** If state taxes collected are $500 or less per month
  - **Annual:** If state taxes collected are less than $200 per quarter
  - Filing frequency is reviewed annually and you will be notified of any changes.
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - **Monthly returns:** Due on or before the last day of the following month
  - **Quarterly returns:** Due on or before the last day of the month following the end of the quarter (e.g., January-March quarter due April 30)
  - **Annual returns:** Due on or before January 31 of the following year
  - When the due date falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or state holiday, the return is due the next business day.
- **Filing portal:** [MyTax Missouri](https://mytax.mo.gov/)

Source: [Sales Tax FAQs | Missouri DOR](https://dor.mo.gov/faq/taxation/business/sales-tax-filing.html)
