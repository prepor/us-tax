# Indiana (IN) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does the state have sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 7.0% (uniform statewide rate, no local sales taxes) ([DOR: Tax Rates, Fees & Penalties - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/tax-rates-fees-and-penalties/))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 7.0% (Indiana does not allow local jurisdictions to impose additional sales tax) ([DOR: Sales Tax - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/sales-tax/))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 or more in gross revenue from sales into Indiana in the current or previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (effective January 1, 2024). Previously, Indiana had a secondary threshold of 200 or more separate transactions, which was removed. ([DOR: Remote Seller FAQs - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/i-am-a/business-corp/business-faq/remote-seller-faqs/))
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2018 (Indiana Code 6-2.5-2-1, enacted via House Enrolled Act 1129, 2017) ([DOR: Remote Seller Information - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/remote-seller-information/))
- **Official nexus information:** [DOR: Remote Seller Information - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/remote-seller-information/)
- **Important note:** The threshold applies regardless of whether any of the $100,000 in revenue was generated by taxable sales. Registration and filing are required even if all sales are believed to be exempt.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register to obtain a Registered Retail Merchant Certificate (RRMC) through InBiz, Indiana's one-stop online business registration portal. ([DOR: Remote Seller - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/i-am-a/business-corp/remote-sellers/))
- **Online registration portal:** [InBiz - Indiana Business Registration](https://inbiz.in.gov/) via [DOR: Remote Seller - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/i-am-a/business-corp/remote-sellers/)
- **Registration fee:** No fee for the RRMC. The certificate updates automatically with no additional charge for renewal.
- **Electronic filing requirement:** All businesses must file and pay sales tax electronically. ([DOR: Sales Tax - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/sales-tax/))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to the 7% sales tax. ([DOR: Sales Tax - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/sales-tax/))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Exempt. Food and food ingredients purchased for home consumption are exempt from sales tax. This does NOT include candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, or prepared food (e.g., sandwiches, soups). ([Sales Tax Information Bulletin #29 - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/files/reference/sib29.pdf))
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 7% rate with no exemption. Indiana does not have a sales tax holiday. (Indiana briefly had a sales tax holiday in 2015 and 2016, but it was not renewed.)
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt. Prescription drugs dispensed by a registered pharmacist are exempt from sales tax. ([Sales Tax Information Bulletin #7 - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/files/sib07.pdf))
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Taxable (unless purchased with a prescription).
- **Official exemption information:** [Indiana General Sales Tax Exemption Certificate (ST-105)](https://forms.in.gov/Download.aspx?id=2717)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. Indiana has a uniform 7% statewide sales tax rate. Cities and counties cannot impose additional sales tax. ([DOR: Sales Tax - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/business-tax/sales-tax/))
- **Note:** Some Indiana counties impose a separate Food and Beverage Tax (1% in many counties), but this applies to prepared food consumed on or off premises, not to remote retail sales of physical goods. ([DOR: Food & Beverage Tax - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/i-am-a/business-corp/food-and-beverage-tax/))
- **Remote seller local tax obligation:** Not applicable - no local sales taxes to collect.

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by the Indiana DOR based on average monthly sales tax liability:
  - **Monthly:** Average monthly liability exceeding $1,000 (due by the 20th of the following month for early filers; 30th for standard filers)
  - **Quarterly:** Average monthly liability between $200 and $1,000 (due by the 20th of the month following the quarter's close)
  - **Annual:** Average monthly liability below $200 or annual collections less than $1,000 (due by January 31 of the following year)
  - Filing frequency is reviewed annually in November and adjusted for the next tax year.
- **Filing portal:** [INTIME (Indiana Taxpayer Information Management Engine)](https://intime.dor.in.gov/) - Indiana's online tax portal, replacing the former INtax system ([DOR: INTIME - Indiana DOR](https://www.in.gov/dor/resources/online-services/intime/))
- **Note:** You must file a return even if you had no sales during the period.
