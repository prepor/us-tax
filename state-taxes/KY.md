# Kentucky (KY) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6% (Kentucky has no local sales taxes)

Source: [Kentucky Sales & Use Tax](https://revenue.ky.gov/Business/Sales-Use-Tax/pages/default.aspx)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 or more in gross receipts from sales into Kentucky
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate sales transactions into Kentucky
- **Measurement period:** Previous or current calendar year (either threshold triggers nexus)
- **Effective date:** July 1, 2018 (HB 487); enforcement began October 1, 2018
- **Official guidance:** [Kentucky Sales and Use Tax Collections by Remote Retailers](https://revenue.ky.gov/News/Pages/Kentucky-Sales-and-Use-Tax-Collections-by-Remote-Retailers-U.S.-Supreme-Court-Ruling.aspx) | [Remote Retailers and Marketplace Providers FAQ](https://taxanswers.ky.gov/Sales-and-Excise-Taxes/Pages/Remote-Retailers-Marketplace-Providers-FAQs.aspx)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through MyTaxes.ky.gov. Click "Register a new account," complete required fields, and go through the verification steps.
- **Registration portal:** [MyTaxes.ky.gov](https://mytaxes.ky.gov/) | [Kentucky Business One Stop](https://onestop.ky.gov/start/Pages/statetax.aspx)
- **Registration fee:** No fee noted for sales tax registration
- **Paper application:** [Kentucky Tax Registration Application (Form 10A100)](https://revenue.ky.gov/Forms/10A100(P)(4-25)_FINAL_locked%20Fill-in.pdf)
- **Registration help:** (502) 564-3306

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Kentucky imposes a 6% sales and use tax on retail sales of tangible personal property.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food for human consumption:** Exempt (food purchased for preparation and consumption at home). Does not include restaurant meals, take-out/to-go food, or prepared food. **Note:** Candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, and food sold through vending machines are excluded from the food exemption and are taxable at 6% (KRS 139.485).
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 6% rate (no exemption)
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt (KRS 139.472, effective since 1971). Includes prosthetic devices and physical aids.
- **Official information:** [Sales & Use Tax page](https://revenue.ky.gov/Business/Sales-Use-Tax/pages/default.aspx) | [Sales and Excise Tax FAQ](https://taxanswers.ky.gov/Sales-and-Excise-Taxes/Pages/Sales-and-Excise-Tax-FAQs.aspx)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. There are no local sales and use taxes in Kentucky. The 6% rate is uniform statewide.
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Not applicable

Source: [Kentucky Sales & Use Tax](https://revenue.ky.gov/Business/Sales-Use-Tax/pages/default.aspx)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly by default. The Department may allow quarterly or annual filing based on the amount of sales tax reported annually. Filing frequencies are adjusted programmatically each June.
- **Filing deadline:** Returns are due on the **20th of the month** following the reporting period (e.g., July return is due August 20th). If the 20th falls on a weekend or holiday, the return is due the next business day.
- **Electronic filing required:** Yes, online filing and payment is mandated for sales and excise tax returns.
- **Filing portal:** [MyTaxes.ky.gov](https://mytaxes.ky.gov/) | [Online Filing Mandate Info](https://revenue.ky.gov/News/Pages/Online-Filing-and-Payment-Mandate-for-Sales-and-Excise-Tax-Returns.aspx)
