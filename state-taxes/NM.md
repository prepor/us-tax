# New Mexico (NM) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

> **Note:** New Mexico does not have a traditional "sales tax." Instead, it imposes a **Gross Receipts Tax (GRT)** on the seller. While economically similar to a sales tax, the GRT is technically a tax on the business's gross receipts, not on the buyer. Sellers may pass the cost on to buyers, but are not required to do so.

## Overview
- **Has sales/gross receipts tax:** Yes
- **State-level GRT rate:** 4.875% (effective July 1, 2025 - June 30, 2026; the state rate changes annually, typically each July) ([Gross Receipts Tax Rates - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/all-nm-taxes/current-historic-tax-rates-overview/gross-receipts-tax-rates/))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 7.22% ([Gross Receipts Location Code and Tax Rate Map](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/governments/gross-receipts-location-code-and-tax-rate-map/))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in taxable gross receipts sourced to New Mexico in the previous calendar year ([Determining Nexus - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/determining-nexus/))
- **Transaction threshold:** None (no transaction count threshold)
- **Effective date:** July 1, 2019, under HB 6 ([Sales Tax Institute - NM Economic Nexus](https://www.salestaxinstitute.com/resources/new-mexico-enacts-economic-and-marketplace-nexus-legislation))
- **Official nexus guidance:** [Determining Nexus - NM Taxation and Revenue](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/determining-nexus/)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply online for a New Mexico Business Tax Identification Number (NMBTIN, formerly CRS ID) through the Taxpayer Access Point (TAP) portal ([Who Must Register - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/who-must-register-a-business/))
- **Registration portal:** [Taxpayer Access Point (TAP)](https://tap.state.nm.us/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register or obtain a Business Tax Identification Number ([Who Must Register - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/who-must-register-a-business/))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. The GRT applies broadly to gross receipts from selling tangible personal property in New Mexico ([Gross Receipts Tax Overview](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/gross-receipts-overview/))
- **Notable exemptions/deductions:**
  - **Groceries/Food:** Receipts from the sale of food (as defined by the federal food stamp program) at a retail food store may be deducted from gross receipts ([FYI-206 - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2021/09/FYI-206.pdf))
  - **Prescription drugs:** Deductible from gross receipts ([FYI-105 - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2022/12/FYI-105.pdf))
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable (no general exemption for clothing; however, there is an annual GRT tax holiday -- moved from August to the last weekend in July starting 2025 per HB 218 -- covering clothing and shoes under $100/item, school supplies under $30, backpacks under $100, computers $1,000 or less, computer accessories $500 or less, and calculators under $200) ([Tax Holiday - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/news-alerts/tax-holiday/) | [FYI-203 - GRT Holiday (PDF)](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/FYI-203.pdf))
- **Official reference:** [FYI-105: Gross Receipts and Compensating Taxes - An Overview](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/2022/12/FYI-105.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can localities add tax?** Yes. Counties and municipalities impose additional local option GRT increments ([Gross Receipts Tax Rates](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/all-nm-taxes/current-historic-tax-rates-overview/gross-receipts-tax-rates/))
- **Range of local rates:** Combined rates (state + local) range from approximately 5.00% to 9.0625%, depending on location ([Gross Receipts Location Code and Tax Rate Map](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/governments/gross-receipts-location-code-and-tax-rate-map/))
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect at the rate applicable to the destination (delivery location) of the goods. New Mexico switched to destination-based sourcing effective July 1, 2021 ([Determining Nexus - NM Tax & Rev](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/determining-nexus/))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, quarterly, or semiannually, depending on average monthly tax liability. Taxpayers whose CRS tax liability averages less than $200/month may file quarterly or semiannually ([Gross Receipts Tax Overview](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/businesses/gross-receipts-overview/))
- **Filing deadlines:** The CRS-1 form and taxes are due on the **25th of the month** following the end of the reporting period ([CRS-1 Form Instructions](https://www.tax.newmexico.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/CRS1-SHORT-Form-Front-and-Back_01-2019_Fillable.pdf))
- **Filing portal:** [Taxpayer Access Point (TAP)](https://tap.state.nm.us/)
