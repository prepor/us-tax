# New York (NY) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.0% ([Find Sales Tax Rates - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/st/rates.htm))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 8.04% (state 4% + average local 4.04%). Combined rates range up to 8.875% depending on jurisdiction ([Publication 718 - Sales Tax Rates by Jurisdiction](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pdf/publications/sales/pub718.pdf))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $500,000 in gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property delivered into New York State ([Registration Requirement for Businesses with No Physical Presence in NYS](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/publications/sales/nexus.htm))
- **Transaction threshold:** More than 100 sales of tangible personal property delivered into the state ([Registration Requirement - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/publications/sales/nexus.htm))
- **Both thresholds must be met:** Yes, both conditions must be satisfied during the immediately preceding four sales tax quarters
- **Effective date:** June 21, 2018 (date of the Wayfair decision). The $500,000 threshold was set by S.B. 6615, signed June 24, 2019, retroactive to June 21, 2018 (originally $300,000) ([Notice N-19-1 - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pdf/notices/n19-1.pdf))
- **Official nexus guidance:** [Registration Requirement for Businesses with No Physical Presence in NYS](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/publications/sales/nexus.htm)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply for a Certificate of Authority through New York Business Express. You must register at least 20 days before you begin making taxable sales in New York State ([How to Register for NYS Sales Tax](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/how_to_register_for_nys_sales_tax.htm))
- **Registration portal:** [Register as a Sales Tax Vendor - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/st/register.htm) (links to New York Business Express)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register for a Certificate of Authority ([Register as a Sales Tax Vendor](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/st/register.htm))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Tangible personal property is generally subject to sales tax unless specifically exempt ([Products, Services, and Transactions Subject to Sales Tax](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/st/subject.htm))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Clothing and footwear:** Items sold for less than $110 per item or pair are exempt from the 4% state sales tax. Some localities — including New York City — also exempt these items from local sales tax, making qualifying clothing and footwear fully tax-free. However, not all localities extend this exemption; in those jurisdictions, the local tax still applies to clothing under $110. See [Publication 718-C](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pdf/publications/sales/pub718c.pdf) for the full list of jurisdictions and their clothing/footwear tax rates. ([Clothing and Footwear Exemption](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/clothing_and_footwear.htm))
  - **Food and groceries:** Most food and food products sold for home consumption are exempt from sales tax. Exceptions include candy, soft drinks, alcoholic beverages, and prepared food ([Listings of Taxable and Exempt Foods](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/listings_of_taxable_and_exempt_food.htm))
  - **Medicine/drugs:** Prescription and over-the-counter drugs and medicines intended for human use are exempt ([Drugstores and Pharmacies](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/drugstores.htm))
- **Official exemption reference:** [Quick Reference Guide for Taxable and Exempt Property and Services](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/quick_reference_guide_for_taxable_and_exempt_property_and_services.htm)

## Local Taxes
- **Can localities add sales tax?** Yes. Cities, counties, and certain districts impose additional local sales taxes ([Sales Tax Rates by Local Taxing Jurisdiction](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/publications/sales/local_rates_current.htm))
- **Range of local rates:** 3% to 4.875% (the Metropolitan Commuter Transportation District adds an additional 0.375%) ([Publication 718 - Sales Tax Rates by Jurisdiction](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pdf/publications/sales/pub718.pdf))
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect both state and local taxes based on the destination (delivery address) of the goods ([Registration Requirement - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/publications/sales/nexus.htm))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Quarterly, part-quarterly (monthly), or annually, as assigned by the Tax Department ([Filing Requirements for Sales and Use Tax Returns](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/filing_requirements_for_sales_and_use_tax_returns.htm))
- **Filing deadlines:** Quarterly returns are due no later than **20 days after the end of the quarter**. New York sales tax quarters are: March 1 - May 31, June 1 - August 31, September 1 - November 30, December 1 - February 28/29 ([Filing Requirements - NY Tax Dept](https://www.tax.ny.gov/pubs_and_bulls/tg_bulletins/st/filing_requirements_for_sales_and_use_tax_returns.htm))
- **Filing portal:** [Sales Tax Web File](https://www.tax.ny.gov/bus/st/stmp.htm)
