# Illinois (IL) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does the state have sales tax?** Yes. Illinois imposes a Retailers' Occupation Tax (ROT), which functions as the state's sales tax.
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.25% on general merchandise ([Sales & Use Taxes - Illinois Department of Revenue](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/rot.html))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies significantly by location; combined rates can range from 6.25% to over 11% depending on local taxes. Use the [MyTax Illinois Tax Rate Finder](https://mytax.illinois.gov/) to determine the exact rate for a destination. ([Tax Rate Database - Illinois.gov](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxrates.html))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 or more in cumulative gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property to Illinois purchasers during the preceding 12-month period
- **Transaction threshold:** Previously 200 or more separate transactions, but **effective January 1, 2026, the 200-transaction threshold has been removed**. Only the $100,000 revenue threshold applies. ([FY 2026-12, Destination-Based ROT Changes - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/bulletins/fy-2026-12.html))
- **When the law took effect:** Initial nexus standards effective October 1, 2018 (P.A. 100-0587); destination-based ROT collection for remote retailers effective January 1, 2021 (Leveling the Playing Field for Illinois Retail Act, P.A. 101-0031 and P.A. 101-0604) ([FY 2024-08 - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/bulletins/fy-2024-08.html))
- **Official nexus information:** [FAQs for Marketplace Facilitators, Marketplace Sellers, and Remote Retailers - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/frequently-asked-questions-faqs-for-marketplace-facilitators-marketplace-sellers-and-remote-retailers.html)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Complete Form REG-1 (Illinois Business Registration Application) electronically through MyTax Illinois. Processing takes approximately 1-2 business days. ([Pub-113, Registering your Business - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/pubs/retailers-overview-of-sales-and-use-tax/registering-your-business.html))
- **Online registration portal:** [MyTax Illinois](https://mytax.illinois.gov/) - select "Register a New Business (Form REG-1)" ([Business Registration - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/businesses/registration.html))
- **Registration fee:** No fee to register ([Business Registration - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/businesses/registration.html))
- **Support contact:** Central Registration Division at (217) 785-3707 or rev.mytaxhelp@illinois.gov

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally taxable at 6.25%. ([Sales & Use Taxes - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/rot.html))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Effective January 1, 2026, the previous 1% reduced state rate on qualifying food/groceries has been eliminated (P.A. 103-0781). Groceries are now subject to the full 6.25% state rate. However, P.A. 103-0781 also authorized municipalities and counties to impose a local 1% grocery tax by ordinance, and more than 600 have done so — meaning many consumers still pay 1% on groceries as a local tax. ([FY 2026-03 - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/bulletins/fy-2026-03.html); [FY 2026-11 - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/bulletins/fy-2026-11.html))
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 6.25% state rate. No general exemption for clothing.
  - **Prescription medicine and medical appliances:** Taxed at a reduced 1% state rate. ([Sales Tax Exemptions FAQs - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/rot/general-information-about-sales-tax-transactions-and-exemptions.html))
  - **Feminine hygiene products:** Exempt from sales tax.
- **Official exemption/rate information:** [PIO-101, Illinois Sales & Use Tax Matrix - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/rot/pio-101.html)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. There are numerous locally-imposed retailers' occupation taxes and service occupation taxes administered by IDOR. Counties, municipalities, and special districts can all impose additional sales taxes. ([Tax Rate Database - Illinois.gov](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxrates.html))
- **Range of local rates:** Local taxes can add 0.25% to approximately 5% on top of the state rate, resulting in combined rates from 6.25% to over 11%.
- **Remote seller collection:** Yes, remote sellers must collect and remit destination-based state AND local retailers' occupation tax. The rate is determined by the ship-to address. Use the [MyTax Illinois Tax Rate Finder](https://mytax.illinois.gov/) to determine the correct combined rate. ([Destination-Based Sales Tax Assistance - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/taxinformation/sales/destination-based-sales-tax-assistance.html))
- **15% undetermined-location rate:** Effective January 1, 2026 (P.A. 104-0006), if a retailer subject to destination-based sourcing fails to provide the information, schedules, or supporting documents necessary to determine the destination of their sales, those gross receipts will be taxed at a 15% rate — higher than any actual combined Illinois rate. This rate may be applied during an audit for any periods under review, including periods before January 2026. Correct sourcing information can be submitted later to have the rate adjusted. ([FY 2026-12 - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/bulletins/fy-2026-12.html))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by IDOR based on average monthly liability:
  - **Monthly:** Average monthly liability greater than $200
  - **Quarterly:** Average monthly liability between $50 and $200
  - **Annual:** Average monthly liability less than $50
  - ([Pub-113, Requirements for Retailers who File Form ST-1 - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/research/publications/pubs/retailers-overview-of-sales-and-use-tax/requirements-for-retailers-who-file-form-st-1.html))
- **Filing deadlines:** Due on the 20th of the month following the end of the reporting period. Annual returns are due January 20 of the following year. ([ST-1 Instructions - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/forms/sales/salesandusetax/st-1-instructions.html))
- **Filing form:** Form ST-1, Sales and Use Tax and E911 Surcharge Return
- **Filing portal:** [MyTax Illinois](https://mytax.illinois.gov/) ([MyTax Illinois Facts - Illinois DOR](https://tax.illinois.gov/programs/mytax.html))
