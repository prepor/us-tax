# Delaware (DE) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** No. Delaware is one of five US states with **no state or local sales tax**.
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 0% (no sales tax)
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 0%
- **Important note:** While Delaware has no sales tax, it does impose a **Gross Receipts Tax** on businesses operating within the state. This is a tax on the seller, not the consumer, and applies to total gross revenues.

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Not applicable.** Since Delaware does not have a sales tax, there are no economic nexus thresholds for sales tax collection purposes.
- **Gross Receipts Tax nexus:** If you are "engaged in business" in Delaware, you may be subject to the Gross Receipts Tax. Online sellers shipping goods to Delaware customers from outside the state generally do **not** need to collect any tax from Delaware buyers.
- **Official info:** [Doing Business in Delaware](https://revenue.delaware.gov/business-tax-forms/doing-business-in-delaware/)
- **Internet sales FAQ:** [Internet Sales FAQs](https://revenue.delaware.gov/frequently-asked-questions/internet-sales-faqs/)

## Tax Registration
- **Sales tax registration:** Not required (no sales tax exists)
- **Gross Receipts Tax registration:** If you establish a business presence in Delaware, you must obtain a Delaware business license and register for Gross Receipts Tax
- **Registration portal:** [Delaware One Stop](https://onestop.delaware.gov/Operate_File)
- **Business registration info:** [Doing Business in Delaware](https://revenue.delaware.gov/business-tax-forms/doing-business-in-delaware/)
- **Registration fees:** Varies by license type; see Delaware Division of Revenue for specific fees

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** No, not from a sales tax perspective. Delaware does not impose a sales tax on any goods.
- **Gross Receipts Tax:** Businesses located in or doing business in Delaware pay Gross Receipts Tax on their total revenues. Rates range from 0.0945% to 1.9914% depending on the type of business activity (up to 2.4218% for petroleum products).
- **Notable exemptions from Gross Receipts Tax:**
  - Wholesale sales where goods are taken out of state (with Form 373 exemption certificate)
  - See [Exemption Certificates](https://revenue.delaware.gov/business-tax-forms/exemption-certificates/) for details
- **Gross Receipts Tax rates by business type:** [Detailed List of Revenue Licenses and Tax Rates](https://revenuefiles.delaware.gov/docs/gr_rates.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. There is no sales tax at any level in Delaware.
- **Range of local rates:** N/A
- **Remote seller obligations:** None for sales tax purposes. Remote sellers shipping into Delaware do not need to collect any sales tax.

## Filing Requirements
- **Sales tax filing:** Not applicable (no sales tax)
- **Gross Receipts Tax filing (if applicable):** Businesses operating in Delaware file Gross Receipts Tax returns monthly
- **Filing portal:** [Delaware One Stop](https://onestop.delaware.gov/Operate_File)

## Key Takeaway for Online Retailers
If you are an online retailer selling physical goods and shipping them to Delaware customers from outside the state, you do **not** need to collect any sales tax or other tax from Delaware buyers. Delaware has no sales tax. The Gross Receipts Tax only applies to businesses operating within Delaware and is paid by the business, not the consumer.
