# Massachusetts (MA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.25%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6.25% (Massachusetts does not allow local sales taxes)

Source: [Massachusetts Tax Rates | Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/massachusetts-tax-rates)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in Massachusetts sales in the prior or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Massachusetts uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2019
- **Link to official nexus law/regulation:** [Remote Seller and Marketplace Facilitator FAQs | Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/remote-seller-and-marketplace-facilitator-faqs)

Additional reference: [830 CMR 64H.1.9: Remote Retailers and Marketplace Facilitators](https://www.mass.gov/regulations/830-CMR-64h19-remote-retailers-and-marketplace-facilitators-0)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through MassTaxConnect. You will need your business's legal and mailing addresses and an EIN (sole proprietors use their SSN). Non-sole-proprietors also need contact information for owners/officers including their titles and SSNs.
- **Official registration portal:** [Register Your Business with MassTaxConnect | Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/register-your-business-with-masstaxconnect)
- **Registration fees:** No fee mentioned in official resources; registration is free through MassTaxConnect.

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to the 6.25% sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Clothing:** Items costing $175 or less are exempt. For items over $175, tax is only charged on the amount exceeding $175. Special athletic/protective clothing is not exempt.
  - **Food/Groceries:** Food for human consumption (not restaurant meals) is exempt, including candy, confectionery, gum, and seeds for growing food.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt.
  - **Restaurant meals:** Taxable at 6.25%.
- **Sales Tax Holiday:** Massachusetts has a permanent annual Sales Tax Holiday (established 2018), typically held the second weekend in August. During the holiday, retail items priced at $2,500 or less per item are exempt from the 6.25% sales tax. Meals, motor vehicles, motorboats, tobacco, telecommunications services, gas, steam, electricity, and marijuana are excluded. Check [Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov) each year for the exact dates.
- **Link to official exemption information:** [Sales and Use Tax | Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/guides/sales-and-use-tax)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. Massachusetts does not permit local sales taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** N/A
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** N/A

Source: [Sales and Use Tax | Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/guides/sales-and-use-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** Required if sales/use tax liability exceeds $100 per year (most businesses)
  - **Annual:** Required if sales/use tax liability is reasonably estimated to be $100 or less for the calendar year
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - Monthly returns are due on or before the 30th day following the month represented by the return
  - Vendors with over $150,000 in cumulative tax liability in the prior year must make advance payments
- **Late penalties:** 1% of the tax due per month for failure to file or late payment, up to a maximum of 25%
- **Filing portal:** [MassTaxConnect](https://mtc.dor.state.ma.us/mtc/_/)

Source: [830 CMR 62C.16.2: Sales and Use Tax Returns and Payments](https://www.mass.gov/regulations/830-CMR-62c162-sales-and-use-tax-returns-and-payments)
