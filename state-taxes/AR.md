# Arkansas (AR) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** ~9.46%
- **Combined rate range:** 6.5% to ~11.5% depending on location

Sources:
- [State Sales & Use Tax Rates - Arkansas DFA](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/sales-use-tax-rates/state-sales-use-tax-rates/)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in sales of tangible personal property, taxable services, digital codes, or specified digital products delivered into Arkansas
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 transactions (either threshold triggers nexus)
- **Measurement period:** Current or previous calendar year
- **Effective date:** July 1, 2019 (Act 822 of the 92nd Legislative Session)
- **Official nexus page:** [Remote Sellers - Arkansas DFA](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/remote-sellers/)

Sources:

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Complete the Arkansas Combined Registration Application through the Arkansas Taxpayer Access Point (ATAP) portal. Remote sellers can also register through the Streamlined Sales Tax Registration System (SSTRS).
- **Registration portal:** [Register for a Tax Account - Arkansas DFA](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/register-for-a-tax-account/)
- **Remote seller registration:** [Register a Remote Seller - Arkansas DFA](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/service/register-a-remote-seller/)
- **ATAP portal:** [https://atap.arkansas.gov](https://atap.arkansas.gov)
- **Registration fee:** $50.00 (paid electronically upon submission)

## Taxable Goods
- **Physical goods generally taxable:** Yes. Tangible personal property is broadly subject to sales and use tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/groceries:** Exempt from state sales tax as of January 1, 2026 (state reduced food tax rate is 0.000%). Local taxes may still apply. Note: candy and soft drinks remain taxable at the full state rate.
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable at the full rate. However, Arkansas holds an annual sales tax holiday (typically in August) during which certain clothing items are exempt from state and local tax.
  - **Agricultural supplies:** Exemptions for livestock, chemicals, feed, machinery, and seed for commercial farmers (Arkansas Code Ann. 26-52-403, 404, 405)
- **Sales tax holiday (2026):** August 1-2, 2026 -- certain clothing and school supplies exempt

## Local Taxes
- **Cities/counties can add local sales tax:** Yes
- **Range of local rates:** City rates range from ~0.5% to 3.0%; county rates range from ~0.5% to 2.0%. Combined local add-ons can be up to ~5.5%.
- **Remote seller local tax obligations:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect applicable local taxes in addition to the state tax. Arkansas uses destination-based sourcing for remote sellers.
- **Local tax lookup tool:** [Sales and Use Tax Section Local Tax Lookup Tools](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/local-tax-lookup-tools/)
- **Rate changes:** [Local Sales & Use Tax Rate Changes](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/recent-changes-in-sales-use-tax/recent-changes-for-local-taxes/)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually (assigned based on tax liability)
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due on the 20th of the month following the reporting period
- **Filing portal:** [Arkansas Taxpayer Access Point (ATAP)](https://atap.arkansas.gov)
- **Electronic filing info:** [Electronic Filing and Payment Options](https://www.dfa.arkansas.gov/office/taxes/excise-tax-administration/sales-use-tax/electronic-filing-and-payment-options/)
- **Zero-filing required:** Yes, even if no tax is due for the period
