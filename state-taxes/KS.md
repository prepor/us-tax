# Kansas (KS) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** ~8.78% (varies by location; can reach up to 10.6% in some jurisdictions)

Sources: [Kansas Sales Tax Rate](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/bustaxtypessales.html) | [Local Rate Locator](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/atrl.html)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in cumulative gross receipts from sales to Kansas customers in the current or preceding calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Kansas uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **Effective date:** July 1, 2021 (Senate Bill 50, amending K.S.A. 79-3702)
- **Official guidance:** [Notice 21-17 - Remote Sellers (PDF)](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/taxnotices/notice21-17.pdf)

A remote seller must register with the Kansas Department of Revenue no later than 30 days after exceeding the $100,000 threshold. Remote sellers may voluntarily collect tax on the first $100,000 of sales for the benefit of their customers.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Kansas Department of Revenue Customer Service Center. Complete the business tax questionnaire to determine applicable registrations.
- **Registration portal:** [Kansas Business Registration](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/busregistration.html) | [Customer Service Center](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/eservices.html)
- **Registration fee:** No fee to register for general sales tax. (Note: the $25/location fee that appears on some forms is for cigarette retailer licenses only, not general sales tax registration.)
- **Additional info:** [Pub. KS-1216 Business Tax Application](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/pub1216.html)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are subject to Kansas sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food and food ingredients:** State sales tax rate on food is **0%** as of January 1, 2025 (local taxes still apply). "Food and food ingredients" includes substances sold for human nourishment, including bottled water, candy, dietary supplements, and soft drinks. Prepared food (heated items, items sold with utensils) is taxed at the full state rate.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full rate (no exemption)
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax
- **Official exemption information:** [Pub. KS-1223 Food Sales Tax Rate Reduction](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/pub1223.html) | [Pub. KS-1520 Kansas Exemptions](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/pdf/pub1520.pdf) | [Exemption Certificates](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/prpecwelcome.html)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes
  - **City tax:** Up to 2.0% for general purposes and up to 1.0% for special purposes (increments of 0.05%)
  - **County tax:** Up to 1.0% (increments of 0.25%), plus an additional 1% for health care services
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to ~4% (combined city and county)
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect the applicable local tax based on destination-based sourcing rules (tax is based on the delivery address).
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Kansas Sales and Use Tax Rate Locator](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/atrl.html) | [Destination-Based Sourcing Rules](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/sourcingrules.html)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Based on tax liability incurred during the preceding calendar year. Filing frequency is assigned by KDOR and may be monthly, quarterly, or annually. A notice of proposed changes is mailed before January 1 each year.
- **Filing deadline:** Returns are due on the **25th of the month** following the end of the reporting period
- **Electronic filing required:** Yes, since July 1, 2010, businesses must submit returns electronically
- **Filing portal:** [KDOR Customer Service Center](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/eservices.html) | [WebFile](https://www.kansas.gov/webfile/) | [E-Filing Options](https://www.ksrevenue.gov/efilingoptions.html)
