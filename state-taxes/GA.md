# Georgia (GA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies by county. Counties impose multiple local sales taxes (each at 1%), resulting in combined rates typically between 7% and 9%. For example, Muscogee County has a combined rate of 8% (4% state + 4% local) and Ware County has 9% (4% state + 5% local).

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales into Georgia in the current or previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate retail transactions in the current or previous calendar year
- **Note:** These are **alternative** thresholds -- exceeding either one triggers the obligation to register and collect tax
- **When the law took effect:** January 1, 2019 (initially at $250,000 / 200 transactions). Threshold lowered to $100,000 / 200 transactions effective **January 1, 2020** via HB 182, signed by Governor Brian Kemp on April 28, 2019.
- **Official nexus information:** [Out-of-State Sellers](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/sales-use-tax/out-state-sellers)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Georgia Tax Center (GTC). After online submission, you should receive your tax account number within 15 minutes by email.
- **Registration portal:** [Georgia Tax Center (GTC)](https://gtc.dor.ga.gov/_/)
- **Registration guidance:** [How to Register a Sales and Use Tax Account](https://dor.georgia.gov/how-register-sales-and-use-tax-account)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register
- **Key details:** Any individual or entity meeting the definition of "dealer" must register regardless of whether all sales are online, out of state, wholesale, or exempt. Registration does not require renewal and remains in effect as long as the business exists with no change in ownership or structure.
- **Registration FAQ:** [Sales and Use Tax Registration - FAQ](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-registration-faq)
- **Required information:** Business legal name, location and mailing address, Federal Identification Number (if applicable)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to Georgia sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Food for home consumption is exempt from the 4% **state** sales tax. However, food remains subject to **all applicable local** sales taxes (typically 3-5%). This means groceries are taxed at local rates only, not the full combined rate.
  - **Clothing:** Generally **taxable** in Georgia (no clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt.
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Taxable. Nonprescription drugs are not included in the sales tax exemptions under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3 and are subject to the full state and local sales tax.
- **Official exemption list:** [List of Sales and Use Tax Exemptions](https://dor.georgia.gov/list-sales-and-use-tax-exemptions)
- **What is subject to tax:** [What is Subject to Sales and Use Tax?](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/sales-use-tax/what-subject-sales-and-use-tax)
- **Food-specific rates:** [Sales Tax Rates - Food, TSPLOST Exempt, and Motor Vehicles](https://dor.georgia.gov/sales-tax-rates-food-tsplost-exempt-and-motor-vehicles)
- **Nontaxable sales:** [Nontaxable Sales](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/nontaxable-sales)
- **Exemption certificate:** [ST-5 Certificate of Exemption](https://dor.georgia.gov/st-5-certificate-exemption)

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Every Georgia county imposes local sales taxes.
- **Types of local taxes:** LOST (Local Option Sales Tax), SPLOST (Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax), TSPLOST (Transportation Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax), ELOST (Educational Local Option Sales Tax), MARTA tax (in metro Atlanta counties), and others. Each is imposed at a 1% rate.
- **Range of local rates:** Typically 3% to 5% on top of the 4% state rate, depending on the county and which local taxes are currently in effect.
- **Remote seller obligations:** Remote sellers must collect both state and local sales taxes based on the delivery destination. Local taxes are reported on the same ST-3 return.
- **Rate charts (updated quarterly):** [Sales Tax Rates - General](https://dor.georgia.gov/sales-tax-rates-general)
- **Current rate chart:** [General Rate Chart - January 1, 2026 through March 31, 2026](https://dor.georgia.gov/document/document/general-rate-chart-effective-january-1-2026-through-march-31-2026/download)
- **Tax rates overview:** [Tax Rates](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/tax-rates)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** The default for most taxpayers
  - **Other frequencies:** Taxpayers may submit a written request to change filing frequency based on total tax liability
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due no later than the **20th day of the month** following the reporting period. Returns must be filed even when no tax is due or no sales were made.
- **Electronic filing requirement:** You are required to file and pay electronically if you owe more than $500.00 on any return
- **Filing portal:** [Georgia Tax Center (GTC)](https://gtc.dor.ga.gov/_/)
- **Filing guidance:** [File & Pay](https://dor.georgia.gov/taxes/business-taxes/sales-use-tax/file-pay)
- **Return form:** [ST-3 Sales and Use Tax Returns and Addendums](https://dor.georgia.gov/st-3-sales-and-use-tax-returns-and-addendums)
- **Filing tips:** [Tips for Completing the Sales and Use Tax Return](https://dor.georgia.gov/tips-completing-sales-and-use-tax-return)
