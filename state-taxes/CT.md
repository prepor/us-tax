# Connecticut (CT) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.35%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6.35% (Connecticut does not allow local sales taxes, so the rate is uniform statewide)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross receipts from sales into Connecticut
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more retail transactions
- **Important: AND logic** — Connecticut requires that **both** thresholds be exceeded (i.e., more than $100,000 in revenue **AND** more than 200 transactions). This differs from most states, which use OR logic. A remote seller exceeding only one threshold is not required to register.
- **Measurement period:** The 12-month period ending on September 30 immediately preceding the monthly or quarterly period for which liability is being determined
- **When the law took effect:** December 1, 2018 (initially at $250,000 / 200 transactions); threshold lowered to $100,000 / 200 transactions effective July 1, 2019
- **Official nexus information:** [Sales and Use Tax Information](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/sales-tax/tax-information)
- **Economic nexus Q&A:** [IP 2010(29.1) Q&A on Economic Nexus](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/publications/informational-publications/2010/ip-2010291-q--a-on-economic-nexus)
- **Legislative summary:** [2019 Regular Session - State Tax Legislation Overview](https://portal.ct.gov/DRS/Legislative-Summaries/2019-Legislative-Updates/Regular-Session-State-Tax-Legislation-Overview)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply online through myconneCT, in person at a DRS local office, or by mail using Form REG-1
- **Registration portal:** [myconneCT](https://portal.ct.gov/drs-myconnect)
- **Registration guidance:** [Applying for a Sales Tax Permit / Resale Number](https://portal.ct.gov/DRS/Sales-Tax/Applying-for-a-Sales-Tax-Permit-Resale-Number)
- **Registration fees:** $100 fee for a Sales and Use Tax Permit. No charge for renewal or replacement.
- **Important:** You must obtain a Sales and Use Tax Permit before making any sales. A temporary permit is assigned and available to print immediately when registering online.
- **More info:** [Registering with DRS](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/businesses/new-business-resource-center/registering-with-drs)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to Connecticut's 6.35% sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Most food products for home consumption are exempt. However, meals, candy, carbonated beverages, and alcoholic beverages are taxable. Bulk food items (whole loaves of bread, whole cakes/pies) are generally exempt; prepared/hot food sold by eating establishments is taxable.
  - **Clothing:** All clothing and footwear are **taxable year-round** at the standard 6.35% rate. Connecticut repealed the year-round clothing exemption in 2011. Items of clothing or footwear priced **over $1,000** are subject to the higher **7.75% luxury tax rate** (applied to the entire sales price, not just the amount over $1,000). The only clothing exemption is during the **annual Sales Tax-Free Week in August** (see below).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt.
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Exempt.
- **Annual Sales Tax-Free Week:** Connecticut holds a Sales Tax-Free Week each August (August 16-22 in 2026). During this week, clothing and footwear items costing **less than $100 per item** are exempt from sales tax. Items $100 or more remain taxable even during the holiday. Athletic/protective clothing primarily designed for specific activities, jewelry, handbags, luggage, umbrellas, wallets, and watches are excluded from the exemption. See [Sales Tax Free Week](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/sales-tax/sales-tax-free-week) for details.
- **Official exemption list:** [Exemptions from Sales and Use Taxes](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/sales-tax/exemptions-from-sales-and-use-taxes)
- **Food guidance:** [Bulletin 31 - Retail Sale of Food and Nonfood Products](https://portal.ct.gov/DRS/Publications/Bulletins/Bulletin-31-Retail-Sale-of-Food-and-Nonfood-Products)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. Connecticut does not impose any local sales taxes. The 6.35% state rate is the only sales tax rate statewide.
- **Range of local rates:** N/A
- **Remote seller obligations for local tax:** N/A

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned by DRS based on tax liability:
  - **Monthly:** If sales tax liability exceeds $4,000 for the prior 12-month period (July 1 to June 30)
  - **Quarterly:** Standard for smaller sellers
  - **Annually:** For very small sellers
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due on or before the **last day of the month** following the end of the reporting period (e.g., January sales are due by the last day of February for monthly filers).
- **Filing portal:** [myconneCT](https://portal.ct.gov/drs-myconnect)
- **Filing information:** [Filing Timely Returns](https://portal.ct.gov/DRS/Sales-Tax/Filing-Timely-Returns)
- **Tax calendar:** [Business Tax Return and Payment Due Dates](https://portal.ct.gov/drs/tax-calendar/business-tax-return-and-payment-due-dates)
