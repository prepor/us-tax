# California (CA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 7.25% (this is the statewide minimum, including state, county, and city base components)
- **Combined average rate (state + local/district):** ~8.98% (per Tax Foundation, 2026)
- **Combined rate range:** 7.25% to 11.25% depending on location (due to voter-approved district taxes)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $500,000 in total combined sales of tangible personal property delivered into California in the preceding or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **Effective date:** April 1, 2019
- **Important note:** The $500,000 threshold includes sales by the retailer and all persons related to the retailer.
- **Collection timing:** The obligation to collect California sales/use tax begins immediately upon the next taxable sale after crossing the $500,000 threshold.
- **Marketplace-facilitated sales:** Sales facilitated through a marketplace (even where the marketplace collects tax on your behalf) count toward the retailer's personal $500,000 economic nexus threshold.
- **Official nexus page:** [Use Tax Collection Requirements Based on Sales into California Due to the Wayfair Decision](https://cdtfa.ca.gov/industry/wayfair/)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Retailers must obtain a California Seller's Permit from the California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA). Registration can be completed online.
- **Registration portal:** [CDTFA Online Registration](https://cdtfa.ca.gov/services/registration.htm)
- **Registration fee:** Registration is free. However, a security deposit may be required depending on the circumstances of your business.
- **Permit duration:** A seller's permit remains valid until it is closed by the holder or revoked by CDTFA.
- **Additional info:** [Do You Need a California Seller's Permit? (Publication 107)](https://cdtfa.ca.gov/formspubs/pub107/)

## Taxable Goods
- **Physical goods generally taxable:** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are taxable unless specifically exempted.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/groceries:** Most food products for human consumption are exempt from sales tax (Revenue and Taxation Code Section 6359). However, this exemption does **not** apply to:
    - Hot prepared food
    - Food sold for on-premises consumption (e.g., restaurant meals)
    - Carbonated beverages
    - Alcoholic beverages
    - Candy and confections
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from sales tax when prescribed for human treatment and dispensed by a registered pharmacist
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Generally taxable unless prescribed by a physician and purchased from a pharmacist
  - **Clothing:** Fully taxable (no exemption)
- **Official exemptions list:** [Common Sales & Use Tax Nontaxable Sales and Partial Exemptions](https://cdtfa.ca.gov/taxes-and-fees/sales-and-use-tax-exemptions.htm)

## Local Taxes
- **District taxes can be added:** Yes. Voters in many California jurisdictions have approved special district taxes on top of the statewide 7.25% base rate.
- **Range of district tax rates:** 0.10% to 2.00% per district. Multiple districts may overlap, resulting in additional total local taxes of up to ~4%.
- **Remote seller local/district tax obligations:** Yes. Out-of-state retailers who meet the $500,000 threshold must collect applicable district (local) use taxes based on the customer's delivery address. California uses destination-based sourcing for use tax.

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned by CDTFA based on reported sales tax or anticipated taxable sales:
  - **Monthly** (with quarterly prepayments for higher-volume sellers)
  - **Quarterly**
  - **Quarterly with prepayments** (prepayments for first two months, then quarterly return)
  - **Annually** or **Fiscal yearly** (for lower-volume sellers)
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - **Quarterly:** Due the last day of the month following the close of the quarter (e.g., Q1 Jan-Mar is due April 30)
  - **Monthly:** Due the last day of the following month
  - **Annually:** Due January 31 for the preceding calendar year
  - If the due date falls on a weekend or state holiday, the deadline extends to the next business day
- **Prepayment requirement:** If on a quarterly prepayment schedule, you must prepay at least 90% of state and local tax liability for the first two months of each quarter
- **Filing portal:** [CDTFA Online Services - File a Return](https://cdtfa.ca.gov/services/file-a-return.htm)
- **Zero-filing required:** Yes, a filing is required even if there are no sales to report
