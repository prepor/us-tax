# Texas (TX) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.25%
- **Combined maximum rate (state + local):** 8.25%. Local jurisdictions (cities, counties, special-purpose districts, and transit authorities) may impose up to an additional 2%.

Sources:
- [Sales and Use Tax - Texas Comptroller](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/)
- [Local Sales and Use Tax FAQ](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/faq/local.php)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $500,000 in total Texas revenue in the preceding 12 calendar months
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Texas uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2019. Remote sellers whose total Texas revenue is less than $500,000 in the preceding 12 calendar months are not required to obtain a tax permit or collect, report, and remit state and local use tax (safe harbor).
- **Compliance timeline:** If a remote seller exceeds the $500,000 safe harbor amount, they must obtain a permit and begin collecting tax no later than the first day of the fourth month after the month they exceeded the threshold.
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - Texas Comptroller](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/remote-sellers.php)

Sources:

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply online using the Texas Online Sales Tax Registration Application, or download and mail Form AP-201 (Texas Application for Sales and Use Tax Permit).
- **Official registration portal:** [Texas Online Tax Registration Application](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/permit/)
- **Registration fees:** There is no fee for the sales tax permit, but you may be required to post a security bond.

Sources:
- [Sales Tax Permit Requirements](https://comptroller.texas.gov/help/sales-tax-registration/requirements.php/)
- [Sales Tax Permit FAQ](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/faq/permit.php)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Most tangible personal property is taxable in Texas.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Exempt. Food products such as flour, sugar, bread, milk, eggs, fruits, vegetables, and similar groceries are not subject to sales tax. However, prepared foods, candy, and soft drinks are taxable.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable (no general clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs/medicine:** Exempt. OTC drugs and medicines with a Drug Facts panel (per FDA regulations) are also exempt (human use only).
  - **Other exempt items:** Wound care dressings and supplies (no prescription needed).
- **Official exemption information:** [Guidelines to Texas Tax Exemptions](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/publications/96-1045.php), [Grocery and Convenience Stores](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/publications/96-280.php), and [Sales Tax Exemptions for Healthcare Items](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/publications/94-155.php)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Cities, counties, special-purpose districts, and transit authorities may impose additional sales and use tax.
- **Range of local rates:** Up to 2% combined local rate (total combined rate cannot exceed 8.25%)
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers must collect local use tax. For 2026, remote sellers may use a single local use tax rate of 1.75% instead of determining the exact local rate at each destination address.
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Sales Tax Rate Locator](https://gis.cpa.texas.gov/)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Determined by the Comptroller after permit approval. May be monthly, quarterly, or annually.
  - **Monthly filers:** Due on the 20th of the month following the reporting period
  - **Quarterly filers:** Due April 20 (Jan-Mar), July 20 (Apr-Jun), October 20 (Jul-Sep), January 20 (Oct-Dec)
  - **Annual filers:** Due January 20 for the previous year
- **Filing deadlines:** The 20th of the month following the reporting period
- **Official filing portal:** [Webfile - Texas Comptroller](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/) (electronic filing through the Comptroller's website)
- **Forms:** [Texas Sales and Use Tax Forms](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/forms/)
