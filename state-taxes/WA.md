# Washington (WA) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Ranges from 6.5% to approximately 10.5% depending on locality. For example, Seattle's combined rate is 10.25%.

Source: [Retail Sales Tax | Washington Department of Revenue](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/retail-sales-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in combined gross receipts sourced or attributed to Washington in the current or prior year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Washington uses a revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** January 1, 2020 (current threshold); earlier rules applied from October 2018
- **What counts toward the threshold:** All Washington income, including retailing, wholesaling, service and other activities, and other apportionable activities
- **Link to official nexus information:** [Out of State Businesses Reporting Thresholds and Nexus | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/education/industry-guides/out-state-businesses-reporting-thresholds-and-nexus)
- **Remote sellers page:** [Remote Sellers | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/retail-sales-tax/marketplace-fairness-leveling-playing-field/remote-sellers)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** File a Business License Application online through My DOR or by mail
- **Registration portal:** [Apply for a Business License | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/open-business/apply-business-license)
- **My DOR account:** Required to file the application and manage your business taxes online
- **Registration fees:** Business license application fee applies (set by the state)
- **What you receive:** A Unified Business Identifier (UBI) / Account ID
- **Processing time:** Approximately 10 business days; city or state endorsements may add 2-3 weeks

Source: [Tax Registration | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/manage-business/state-endorsements/tax-registration)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Groceries / food for home consumption:** Exempt from sales tax. See [When to Charge Sales Tax on a Food Item | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/education/industry-guides/convenience-stores/when-charge-sales-tax-food-item)
  - **Prepared food, soft drinks, dietary supplements:** Taxable
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt
  - **Clothing:** Taxable (no general clothing exemption)
- **Link to official exemption list:** [Retail Sales and Use Tax Exemptions | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/retail-sales-tax/retail-sales-and-use-tax-exemptions)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes, extensively. Local taxes are layered on top of the 6.5% state rate.
- **Range of local rates:** Approximately 0% to 4%, resulting in combined rates from 6.5% to about 10.5%
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes, remote sellers must collect sales tax at the rate based on where the customer receives the goods or services (destination-based sourcing)
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Sales Tax Rate Lookup | WA DOR](https://webgis.dor.wa.gov/taxratelookup/SalesTax.aspx)
- **Local rate tables:** [Local Sales & Use Tax Rate Table | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/sales-use-tax-rates/local-sales-use-tax/local-sales-use-tax-rate-table)
- **Rates are updated quarterly**

Source: [Sales & Use Tax Rates | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/sales-use-tax-rates)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Assigned by the Department of Revenue based on tax liability:
  - Monthly
  - Quarterly (due end of the month following the quarter, e.g., Q1 due April 30)
  - Annual (due April 15)
- **Filing deadlines:** [Filing Frequencies & Due Dates | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/file-pay-taxes/filing-frequencies-due-dates)
- **Filing portal:** [File & Pay Taxes | WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/file-pay-taxes)
- **Note:** Washington also requires Business & Occupation (B&O) tax reporting in addition to sales tax
