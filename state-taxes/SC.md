# South Carolina (SC) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Ranges from 6% to 9%, depending on the county. Most local taxes are 1%, making 7% the most common combined rate.

Sources: [South Carolina Sales Tax](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax), [Local Sales Taxes](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/local-sales-taxes)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales of tangible personal property, products transferred electronically, and services delivered into South Carolina in the previous or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2018. Remote sellers meeting the economic nexus standard on or after this date must obtain a retail license and begin remitting South Carolina Sales and Use Tax by the first day of the second calendar month after nexus is established.
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - SC DOR](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax/remote-sellers), [Nexus - SC DOR](https://dor.sc.gov/businesses/nexus), [Remote Seller FAQs (PDF)](https://dor.sc.gov/resources-site/lawandpolicy/Documents/RemoteSellerFAQ.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply for a Retail License on MyDORWAY by completing a Business Tax Application. Remote sellers with economic nexus are required to obtain a Retail License.
- **Registration portal:** [MyDORWAY](https://mydorway.dor.sc.gov/_/), [Apply for a Business Tax Account](https://dor.sc.gov/businesses/apply-business-tax-account)
- **Registration fees:** $50.00 for a Retail License

Sources: [Licensing (Retail License)](https://dor.sc.gov/businesses/apply-business-tax-account/licensing-retail-license), [MyDORWAY](https://dor.sc.gov/mydorway)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. South Carolina imposes a 6% state Sales and Use Tax on all sales of tangible personal property delivered into the state, unless an exemption or exclusion applies.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Unprepared food (groceries):** Exempt from the state 6% Sales and Use Tax. However, local Sales and Use Taxes may still apply depending on the type of local tax imposed.
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable. No permanent clothing exemption. Clothing is exempt only during the annual Sales Tax Holiday (first Friday through Sunday in August).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax.
  - **Durable medical equipment:** Various medical-related exemptions exist.
- **Official exemptions information:** [Sales Tax Exemptions - SC DOR](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax-exemptions), [SC Sales Tax Manual (PDF)](https://dor.sc.gov/resources-site/lawandpolicy/Documents/SC%20Sales%20Tax%20Manual.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Counties can impose various local sales and use taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 3%. Most local taxes are currently 1%.
- **Types of local taxes:** Capital Projects, Education Capital Improvement, Local Option Sales & Use, School District, Tourism Development, Transportation, County Green Space, and Catawba Tribal Tax.
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers licensed with South Carolina must collect and remit applicable local Sales and Use Taxes. State and local taxes are reported on a single return. Local tax is based on the point of delivery (destination).

Sources: [Local Sales Taxes - SC DOR](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/local-sales-taxes), [Remote Sellers](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax/remote-sellers)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly for remote sellers. The SC DOR may authorize certain businesses to file quarterly, seasonally, or annually based on their tax liability.
- **Filing deadlines:** Payment of sales tax is due no later than the 20th of the month following the month in which the sales were made.
- **Electronic filing requirement:** Taxpayers whose South Carolina tax liability is $15,000 or more per filing period must file and pay electronically.
- **Filing portal:** [MyDORWAY](https://mydorway.dor.sc.gov/_/)
- **Voluntary collection discount:** Remote sellers not required to collect but who voluntarily register may receive a discount of up to $10,000 per fiscal year.

Sources: [Sales Tax - SC DOR](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax), [Remote Sellers](https://dor.sc.gov/sales-use-tax-index/sales-tax/remote-sellers), [Tax Due Dates - SC Business One Stop](https://scbos.sc.gov/business-compliance/taxes-and-credits/tax-due-dates)
