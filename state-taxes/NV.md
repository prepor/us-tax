# Nevada (NV) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does Nevada have a sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.85%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies by county; ranges from 6.85% (state minimum) to 8.375% (Clark County). Most counties fall between 6.85% and 8.375%.

Source: [Sales Tax & Use Tax - Nevada Department of Taxation](https://tax.nv.gov/tax-types/sales-tax-use-tax/)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** More than $100,000 in retail sales into Nevada in the previous or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate retail transactions for delivery into Nevada in the previous or current calendar year
- **When the law took effect:** October 1, 2018 (following the South Dakota v. Wayfair Supreme Court decision of June 21, 2018)
- **Registration timing:** Once a remote seller hits the threshold, they must register by the first day of the calendar month that begins at least 30 calendar days after they hit the threshold. Sales tax collection begins on that date.
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - Wayfair Decision](https://tax.nv.gov/faqs/marketplace-facilitator-seller-faqs/remote-sellers-wayfair-decision/)

> **Verification note (March 2026):** Some third-party tax compliance sites (e.g., nexusaccountant.com) claim Nevada eliminated the 200-transaction threshold effective January 1, 2024. However, the official Nevada DOT FAQ page, Avalara, TaxJar, and TaxCloud all continue to list both thresholds. The Avalara article specifically listing states that have eliminated transaction thresholds does not include Nevada. No specific legislation repealing the transaction threshold was identified. The thresholds listed above reflect the current official guidance. Recommend verifying directly with the Nevada Department of Taxation (866-962-3707) if this is critical to a compliance decision.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:**
  - **Option 1 (recommended for remote sellers):** Register through the [Streamlined Sales & Use Tax Registration System](https://www.streamlinedsalestax.org/), which allows sellers to register for all Streamlined member states in one application.
  - **Option 2:** Register directly with Nevada using [My Nevada Tax](https://mynvtax.nv.gov/tap)
  - **Option 3:** Complete the Nevada Business Registration form (Form TAX-F006) by mail.
- **Official registration portal:** [My Nevada Tax](https://mynvtax.nv.gov/tap)
- **Registration fees:** $15.00 per sales tax permit

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Most tangible personal property transferred for value is subject to Nevada sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Unprepared food/groceries:** Exempt from sales tax
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax
  - **Clothing:** Taxable (Nevada does not exempt clothing)
  - **Farm machinery and equipment:** Exempt
  - **Newspapers:** Exempt
- **Prepared food** (restaurant meals, heated food) is taxable.

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Nevada's sales tax is composed of state and county/local components. Counties levy additional taxes on top of the 6.85% state rate.
- **Range of local rates:** Combined rates (state + local) range from 6.85% to 8.375%, depending on the county. Notable rates:
  - Clark County (Las Vegas): 8.375%
  - Washoe County (Reno): 8.265%
  - Other counties vary (see the official rate component document)
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers must collect the sales tax rate applicable to the delivery address. Use Nevada's Sales Tax Map to determine the correct rate for each county.
- **Rate components document:** [Components of Sales and Use Tax Rates (PDF)](https://tax.nv.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Components-of-Sales-Tax.pdf)
- **County rate map:** [County Map of Nevada - Sales Tax](https://tax.nv.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/ADM-T047-State-Sales-Tax-Map.pdf)

Source: [Sales and Use Tax Publications](https://tax.nv.gov/Publications/Sales_and_Use_Tax_Publications/)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, quarterly, or annually (assigned by the Department based on tax liability)
- **Filing deadlines:** Effective January 2026, returns are due by the **20th day of the month** following the reporting period. (Prior to 2026, the deadline was the last day of the following month.)
- **Filing portal:** [My Nevada Tax](https://mynvtax.nv.gov/tap)
- **Paper forms:** [Current Sales and Use Tax Forms](https://tax.nv.gov/Forms/salesAndUseTaxForms/)
