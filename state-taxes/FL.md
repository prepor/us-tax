# Florida (FL) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies by county. The state rate of 6% is supplemented by county-level discretionary sales surtaxes ranging from 0% to 2.5%, resulting in combined rates typically between 6% and 8%.

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in taxable remote sales of tangible personal property into Florida in the previous calendar year (only taxable sales count toward the threshold; exempt sales and non-tangible services are excluded)
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Florida uses only a revenue threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2021
- **Key details:** Businesses making remote sales into Florida must collect and electronically remit sales and use tax, including any applicable discretionary sales surtax, once they exceed the $100,000 threshold. Marketplace sellers may exclude sales facilitated through a marketplace provider when calculating whether they meet the threshold.
- **Official nexus information:** [Information for Out-of-State Businesses](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/businesses/Pages/outstate.aspx)
- **Tax Information Publication:** [TIP 21A01-03 (PDF)](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/tips/Documents/TIP_21A01-03.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply online using the Florida Business Tax Application through the Department of Revenue's online portal
- **Registration portal:** [Online Taxpayer Application](https://taxapps.floridarevenue.com/taxregistration)
- **Registration guidance:** [Account Management and Registration](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/eservices/Pages/registration.aspx)
- **Application form (PDF):** [Florida Business Tax Application (DR-1)](https://floridarevenue.com/forms_library/current/dr1.pdf)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to register as a sales tax dealer
- **Important:** You must register as a sales and use tax dealer before you begin conducting business in Florida. After registration, you will receive a Certificate of Registration and an Annual Resale Certificate.
- **Instructions:** [Registering Your Business (DR-1N)](https://www.floridarevenue.com/Forms_library/current/dr1n.pdf)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to Florida's 6% state sales tax plus applicable discretionary sales surtax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Most grocery food items for home consumption are exempt from sales tax. Prepared food, hot food, and food sold for on-premises consumption are taxable. See the [Nontaxable Medical Items and General Grocery List (DR-46NT)](https://floridarevenue.com/forms_library/current/dr46nt.pdf) for the full list.
  - **Clothing:** Generally **taxable** in Florida (no general clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt.
  - **Over-the-counter medicine and medical items:** Many are exempt; see [DR-46NT](https://floridarevenue.com/forms_library/current/dr46nt.pdf) for the complete list of nontaxable medical items.
  - **Oral hygiene products:** Exempt (toothbrushes, toothpaste, dental floss, mouthwash, etc.), effective July 1, 2023 (ch. 2023-157, Laws of Florida).
- **Official exemption information:** [Florida Sales and Use Tax](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/taxesfees/Pages/sales_tax.aspx)
- **Exemption certificates:** [Sales Tax Exemption Certificates](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/businesses/Pages/sales_cex.aspx)
- **New exemptions (2025):** [TIP 25A01-05 - New Sales Tax Exemptions Beginning August 1, 2025](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/tips/Documents/TIP_25A01-05.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Florida counties may impose a Discretionary Sales Surtax (also called local option county tax).
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 2.5% (statutory maximum), though no county currently levies the full 2.5%. The current highest county surtax rate is 2%, making the highest combined rate approximately 8%.
- **Key details:** The surtax applies to the first $5,000 of the sales amount on tangible personal property. New surtax rates become effective January 1 each year.
- **Remote seller obligations:** Remote sellers must collect the applicable discretionary sales surtax based on the delivery address of the customer. The surtax is reported on the same sales tax return.
- **Surtax rate table:** [Discretionary Sales Surtax Rate Table](https://pointmatch.floridarevenue.com/General/DiscretionarySalesSurtaxRates.aspx)
- **Surtax information:** [Discretionary Sales Surtax](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/taxesfees/Pages/discretionary.aspx)
- **Annual rate publication:** [Discretionary Sales Surtax Information (DR-15DSS)](https://floridarevenue.com/Forms_library/current/dr15dss.pdf)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly or Quarterly:** Most new businesses start with quarterly filing; filing frequency may change based on the amount of tax collected
  - Filing frequency is determined by the Department based on your tax liability
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due on the **1st of the month** following each reporting period and are **late after the 20th**. If the 20th falls on a weekend or holiday, the due date is the next business day.
- **Late penalties:** 10% of the amount of tax owed (minimum $50, even if no tax is due)
- **Filing portal:** [eFile and Pay Sales and Use Tax](https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/eservices/Pages/sutlogin.aspx)
- **Return instructions:** [Instructions for DR-15 Sales and Use Tax Return](https://floridarevenue.com/Forms_library/current/dr15n.pdf)
