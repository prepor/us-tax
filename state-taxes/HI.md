# Hawaii (HI) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does the state have sales tax?** Yes, but Hawaii uses a **General Excise Tax (GET)** instead of a traditional sales tax. The GET is levied on the gross income of businesses rather than being a tax on the consumer, though businesses are allowed to pass the tax on to customers.
- **State-level GET rate:** 4.0% ([General Excise Tax (GET) Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/get/))
- **Combined rate (state + county surcharge):** 4.712% in all four counties (Honolulu, Hawaii, Kauai, and Maui) as of 2025-2026 ([County Surcharge on GET - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/countysurcharge/))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from Hawaii sales in the current or previous year
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate transactions in the current or previous year
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2018 (Act 41, S.B. 2514, Laws 2018), codified at Hawaii Revised Statutes Section 237-2.5 ([Tax Announcement 2018-10 (Amended) - Hawaii DOTAX](https://files.hawaii.gov/tax/news/announce/ann18-10_amended.pdf))
- **Official nexus information:** [Tax Information Release No. 2019-03 (Revised)](https://files.hawaii.gov/tax/legal/tir/tir19-03_rev2.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Complete Form BB-1 (State of Hawaii Basic Business Application) online through Hawaii Tax Online or in person at any DOTAX district office ([Licensing Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/licensing/))
- **Online registration portal:** [Hawaii Tax Online](https://hitax.hawaii.gov/) via [E-Services - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/eservices/)
- **Registration fee:** One-time $20 fee ([Licensing Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/licensing/))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. The GET applies to virtually all business activity, including the sale of tangible personal property. ([General Excise Tax (GET) Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/get/))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** NOT exempt. Groceries are subject to GET at the full rate. Hawaii is one of the few states that taxes groceries without a reduced rate. ([General Excise Tax (GET) Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/get/))
  - **Clothing:** NOT exempt. Clothing is taxable under GET.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from GET. Amounts received for selling prescription drugs or prosthetic devices to an individual by a hospital, infirmary, medical clinic, health care facility, pharmacy, or licensed practitioner are exempt under HRS Section 237-24.3(6). This exemption does not extend to services provided in connection with selling those drugs.
  - **SNAP/WIC purchases:** Exempt from GET ([GET Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/get/))
  - **Sales shipped out of state:** Exempt for manufacturers/producers/sellers shipping to a point outside the state where goods are resold or consumed outside Hawaii
- **Official exemption information:** [General Excise and Use Tax Exemptions - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/stats/a5_1annual/a5_7ge_use/)

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties add local tax?** Yes. Counties may impose a surcharge on top of the state GET rate. ([County Surcharge on GET - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/countysurcharge/))
- **County surcharge rates (as of 2025-2026):**
  - City and County of Honolulu: 0.5% surcharge (effective January 1, 2007 - December 31, 2030)
  - County of Hawaii: 0.5% surcharge (effective January 1, 2020 - December 31, 2030)
  - County of Kauai: 0.5% surcharge (effective January 1, 2019 - December 31, 2030)
  - County of Maui: 0.5% surcharge (effective January 1, 2024 - December 31, 2030)
- **Combined effective rate with surcharge:** 4.712% in all counties (the visibly passed-on rate to consumers)
- **Remote seller collection:** Yes, remote sellers with economic nexus must collect the county surcharge in addition to the base GET rate. ([County Surcharge on GET - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/countysurcharge/))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, quarterly, or semi-annual (assigned based on tax liability) ([General Excise Tax (GET) Information - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/get/))
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - Periodic returns (monthly, quarterly, semi-annual): Due on the 20th day of the month following the close of the tax period
  - Annual reconciliation return: Due on the 20th day of the fourth month following the close of the taxable year (April 20 for calendar year filers)
- **E-filing requirement:** Mandatory for taxpayers with annual GET liability exceeding $4,000 ([Mandatory Electronic Filing - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/geninfo/efile-mandate/))
- **Filing portal:** [Hawaii Tax Online](https://hitax.hawaii.gov/) via [E-Services - Hawaii DOTAX](https://tax.hawaii.gov/eservices/)
