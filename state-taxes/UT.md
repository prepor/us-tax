# Utah (UT) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.85%. This is composed of mandatory statewide components: 1.75% (state) + 1.0% (local option) + 0.25% (county option) + additional statewide levies. Additional local components (mass transit, highway, rural hospital, arts & zoo, town option, resort taxes, etc.) vary by jurisdiction.
- **Combined rate range (state + local):** 6.10% to 9.05% for most jurisdictions (as of Q1 2026). Some resort communities and tribal jurisdictions may have combined rates exceeding 9.05%.
- **Grocery food rate:** 3.0% statewide (1.75% state + 1.0% local option + 0.25% county option). Note: SB0122 (2025 session) proposed eliminating the 1.75% state portion of the food tax, but the bill did not pass. The 3.0% reduced rate on grocery food remains in effect.

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales of tangible personal property, electronically transferred products, or services into Utah in the previous or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective July 1, 2025; only the $100,000 revenue threshold applies)
- **When the law took effect:** January 1, 2019
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - Utah State Tax Commission](https://tax.utah.gov/sales/remote-sellers) and [Out-of-State (Remote) Sellers](https://tax.utah.gov/business/sales-tax/other-sales-tax/out-of-state-remote-sellers/)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through TAP (Taxpayer Access Point), Utah's official online tax portal.
- **Official registration portal:** [TAP - Taxpayer Access Point](https://tap.utah.gov/)
- **Registration FAQ:** [TAP FAQ - Registration](https://tax.utah.gov/training/tap-faq/registration)
- **Registration fees:** Not specified in official sources. Contact the Utah State Tax Commission for confirmation.
- **Streamlined Sales Tax (SST):** Utah is a member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Remote sellers may also register through the [SST Registration System](https://tax.utah.gov/sales/sst).

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Utah taxes most tangible personal property.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Taxable, but at a reduced combined rate of 3.0% statewide (instead of the full combined rate). SB0122 (2025 session) proposed eliminating the 1.75% state portion, but the bill did not pass; the 3.0% rate remains in effect. Grocery food does not include alcoholic beverages, tobacco, or prepared food.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full combined rate (no clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax.
  - **Medical equipment:** Durable medical equipment and disposable home medical equipment are exempt with a prescription.
- **Official exemption information:** [Pub 25 - Sales and Use Tax General Information (PDF)](https://files.tax.utah.gov/tax/forms/pubs/pub-25.pdf) and [Pub 53 - Sales Tax for Healthcare Providers](https://tax.utah.gov/forms-pubs/pub-53/)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Utah has multiple layers of local taxes including local option, county option, mass transit, highway, rural hospital, arts & zoo, town option, resort, and impacted communities taxes.
- **Range of combined rates:** 6.10% to 9.05% for most jurisdictions (as of Q1 2026); some resort communities and tribal jurisdictions may exceed this range
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. Remote sellers must collect the full combined rate applicable to the delivery destination. Rates are determined by ZIP+4 code.
- **Quarterly rate changes:** [Quarterly Sales Tax Rate Changes](https://tax.utah.gov/sales/ratechanges)

Sources:

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** Required if annual sales and use tax liability is $50,000 or more. Due by the last day of the month following the reporting period (e.g., July sales due by August 31).
  - **Quarterly/Annual:** Available for sellers with lower tax liability. Contact the Tax Commission for assignment.
- **Seller discount:** Sellers who file and pay monthly returns timely may qualify for a 1.31% seller discount on certain sales and use taxes.
- **Official filing portal:** [TAP - Taxpayer Access Point](https://tap.utah.gov/)
- **Filing help:** [TAP FAQ Help for Sales Tax](https://tax.utah.gov/online-services/tap-salestax/) and [Monthly Filing & Paying](https://tax.utah.gov/sales/monthly)
