# Alaska (AK) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has state sales tax:** No. Alaska is one of five states with no state-level sales tax.
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 0%
- **Local sales tax:** Yes -- over 100 local jurisdictions impose their own sales taxes
- **Local rate range:** 1% to 7.5%
- **Statewide average local rate:** ~1.82%

Sources:
- [Alaska Tax Facts - Office of the State Assessor](https://www.commerce.alaska.gov/web/dcra/officeofthestateassessor/alaskataxfacts.aspx)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales into Alaska (current or previous calendar year)
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was repealed effective January 1, 2025)
- **Effective date:** The Alaska Remote Seller Sales Tax Commission (ARSSTC) was established in 2019. The economic nexus rules apply to remote sellers shipping to participating jurisdictions.
- **Important note:** Economic nexus only applies in ARSSTC member jurisdictions. Non-member jurisdictions cannot require remote sellers to collect tax.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Remote sellers who meet the $100,000 threshold must register with the ARSSTC within 30 days of meeting the threshold.
- **Registration portal:** [ARSSTC Filing Portal](https://arsstc.munirevs.com/)
- **Registration fees:** No fee
- **Permit renewal:** Not required; permits are issued immediately upon registration

Sources:
- [Business/Sellers - ARSSTC](https://arsstc.org/business-sellers/)

## Taxable Goods
- **Physical goods generally taxable:** Yes, in jurisdictions that impose a local sales tax. Tangible personal property is broadly taxable.
- **Notable exemptions:** Exemptions vary by local jurisdiction. Each ARSSTC member community maintains its own exemption list. However:
  - Most Alaskan communities do **not** exempt food/groceries from sales tax
  - WIC and SNAP purchases are exempt per federal rules in all jurisdictions
  - Prescription drugs may or may not be exempt depending on the local jurisdiction
  - Clothing is generally taxable
- **Official exemption info:** Exemptions are governed by each local jurisdiction's ordinance. The ARSSTC provides a unified code, but individual community exemptions may apply.

## Local Taxes
- **Cities/boroughs can add local sales tax:** Yes. This is the only form of sales tax in Alaska.
- **Range of local rates:** 1% to 7.5%
- **Number of taxing jurisdictions:** Over 100 of Alaska's 165 communities impose a local sales tax
- **Remote seller obligations:** Remote sellers must collect tax for all ARSSTC member jurisdictions based on destination (buyer's address). Alaska uses destination-based sourcing.
- **Notable:** Anchorage and Fairbanks do not charge a local sales tax.
- **Member jurisdictions list:** [ARSSTC Member Jurisdictions](https://arsstc.org/business-sellers/member-jurisdictions/)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly or quarterly (assigned upon registration)
  - **Monthly returns:** Due the last day of the following month
  - **Quarterly returns:**
    - Q1 (Jan-Mar): Due April 30
    - Q2 (Apr-Jun): Due July 31
    - Q3 (Jul-Sep): Due October 31
    - Q4 (Oct-Dec): Due January 31
- **Zero-filing required:** Yes. A return must be filed even if no tax is due.
- **Filing portal:** [ARSSTC Filing Portal](https://arsstc.munirevs.com/)
