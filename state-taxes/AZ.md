# Arizona (AZ) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes. Arizona calls its sales tax the **Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)**. Technically, TPT is a tax on the seller for the privilege of doing business, not a traditional sales tax -- but it functions similarly for compliance purposes.
- **State-level TPT rate:** 5.6%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** ~8.52%
- **Combined rate range:** 5.6% to ~11.2% depending on location

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales to Arizona customers in the current or previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **Effective date:** October 1, 2019
- **Official nexus page:** [Economic Threshold - AZDOR](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax/retail-sales-subject-tpt/out-state-sellers/economic-threshold)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Out-of-state remote sellers must obtain a TPT license from the Arizona Department of Revenue.
- **Registration portal:** [TPT License - AZDOR](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax/tpt-license)
- **Registration fee:** $12 for the initial TPT license. The license is valid for the calendar year in which it is issued and may be renewed for subsequent years at no additional cost.
- **License renewal:** TPT licenses must be renewed annually. The 2026 renewal deadline was January 1, 2026.
- **Licensing requirements for remote sellers:** [Licensing and Renewal Requirements - Out-of-State Sellers](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax/retail-sales-subject-tpt/out-state-sellers/licensing-and-renewal)

## Taxable Goods
- **Physical goods generally taxable:** Yes
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/groceries:** Most food items purchased at a grocery store are exempt from state TPT (however, some local jurisdictions may tax food)
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from both state and city TPT
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable (no exemption)
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Generally taxable
- **Official exemptions page:** [TPT Exemptions - AZDOR](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax/tpt-exemptions)

## Local Taxes
- **Cities/counties can add local TPT:** Yes. Most Arizona cities and some counties impose additional TPT on top of the state rate.
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to ~5.3%, with an average local add-on of ~2.8%
- **Remote seller local tax obligations:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect and remit local TPT based on the customer's shipping address (destination-based sourcing). Arizona provides a single consolidated return covering state, county, and city TPT.
- **Rate lookup tool:** [Tax Rate Table - AZDOR](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax/tax-rate-table)
- **Rate table download (Jan 2026):** [TPT Rate Table 01/01/2026 (PDF)](https://azdor.gov/sites/default/files/document/TPT_RATETABLE_01012026.pdf)

**Two-Level Tax Rate Note:** Some Arizona jurisdictions opt in to a two-level retail TPT rate structure. In participating jurisdictions, for 2026 the threshold for the higher rate on single-item sales is $14,338 (adjusted from $13,886). Sales of individual items above this amount are taxed at a lower rate on the portion exceeding the threshold. This is a per-jurisdiction opt-in and is not universal across all Arizona localities.

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Based on estimated annual combined TPT liability:
  - **Annual:** Less than $2,000 estimated annual tax liability
  - **Quarterly:** $2,000 - $8,000 estimated annual tax liability
  - **Monthly:** More than $8,000 estimated annual tax liability
- **Filing deadlines:** TPT is due on the 20th of the month following the end of the tax period. However:
  - **Electronic returns:** Timely if received by the last calendar day of the month in which they are due (by 11:59 p.m.)
  - **Paper returns:** Timely if received by the second-to-last business day of the month in which they are due
- **Filing portal:** [AZTaxes.gov](https://aztaxes.gov) (Arizona's online filing portal)
- **Filing frequency change:** Must be requested via Form 10193 (cannot be done online)
