# Vermont (VT) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6%
- **Combined rate (state + local):** 6% in most areas; 7% in municipalities that have adopted the 1% local option tax

Sources:
- [Sales and Use Tax - VT Department of Taxes](https://tax.vermont.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax)
- [Tax Rates and Charts](https://tax.vermont.gov/research-and-reports/tax-rates-and-charts)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in sales in the preceding 12-month period
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was repealed effective July 1, 2022; Vermont is now revenue-only)
- **Measurement period:** At the end of each quarter, sellers must review their sales over the prior 12 months to determine if they met the revenue threshold. If met, they must begin collecting and remitting tax no later than the first day of the following month.
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2018
- **Click-through nexus:** Also applies when a remote seller has an agreement with a Vermont resident to refer customers and makes more than $10,000 in taxable sales in Vermont in the previous year.
- **Official nexus information:** [Sales Tax and Wayfair FAQ](https://tax.vermont.gov/business-and-corp/sales-and-use-tax/wayfair/faqs) and [Click Through Nexus](https://tax.vermont.gov/business-and-corp/sales-and-use-tax/click-through-nexus)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register for a Vermont Business Tax Account and license prior to collecting tax. Registration is free. Out-of-state vendors can register through myVTax.
- **Official registration portal:** [myVTax](https://myvtax.vermont.gov/_/)
- **Getting started guide:** [Sales and Use Tax: Getting Started](https://tax.vermont.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax/getting-started)
- **Registration fees:** Free

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Most tangible personal property is subject to Vermont's 6% sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Exempt. Food, food products, and beverages are exempt from sales tax. Exception: soft drinks and alcoholic beverages are taxable.
  - **Clothing:** Exempt. Clothing and shoes are not subject to Vermont sales tax.
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt. Drugs intended for human consumption are exempt (product-based exemption -- exempt regardless of intended use).
  - **Over-the-counter drugs:** Exempt.
  - **Medical equipment and supplies:** Exempt (including prescription eyeglasses and contact lenses).
  - **Menstrual care products:** Exempt (menstrual cups, pads, tampons, panty liners, etc.).
- **Official exemption information:** [What is Taxable and Exempt](https://tax.vermont.gov/exempt-items), [General Guidelines on Sales Tax (PDF)](https://tax.vermont.gov/sites/tax/files/documents/FS-1028.pdf), and [Sales and Use Tax Exemptions](https://tax.vermont.gov/business/sales-and-use-tax/exemptions)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Municipalities may levy a 1% local option tax on sales (destination-based).
- **Range of local rates:** 0% or 1% (municipalities either adopt the 1% local option or do not)
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. All vendors, including out-of-state vendors authorized to collect taxes in Vermont, are required to collect and remit applicable local option taxes for sales delivered to participating municipalities. Taxpayers with sales subject to local option tax must file electronically.
- **Administration:** Although levied by municipalities, the local option tax is collected and administered by the Vermont Department of Taxes.
- **Official local option tax info:** [Local Option Tax](https://tax.vermont.gov/business/local-option-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Based on annual sales and use tax liability from the immediately preceding calendar year:
  - **Monthly:** If annual liability exceeds $500
  - **Quarterly:** If annual liability is $500 or less
- **Filing deadlines:**
  - **Monthly filers:** Due by the 25th of the month following the reporting month (exception: February 23rd)
  - **Quarterly filers:** Due April 25, July 25, October 25, and January 25
- **Official filing portal:** [myVTax](https://myvtax.vermont.gov/_/)
- **Filing info:** [File and Pay](https://tax.vermont.gov/business/file-and-pay)
