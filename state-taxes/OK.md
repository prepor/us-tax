# Oklahoma (OK) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.5%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Ranges from 4.5% to 11.5%; major cities like Tulsa have a combined rate of approximately 8.517%

Sources: [Oklahoma Sales and Use Tax](https://oklahoma.gov/tax/businesses/sales-use-tax.html)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in aggregate sales of tangible personal property delivered into Oklahoma in the preceding or current calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (revenue-only threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** Oklahoma's remote seller law (HB 1019, Laws 2018, Second Extraordinary Session) originally took effect July 1, 2018 with a $10,000 threshold. The current $100,000 threshold became effective November 1, 2019.
- **Official nexus information:** [Oklahoma Wayfair FAQs (PDF)](https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/tax/documents/resources/publications/streamlines-sales-tax/WayfairFAQs-06152020.pdf)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through OkTAP (Oklahoma Taxpayer Access Point). Select "Register" then "Register a Business," and choose "Register or add a new sales/use/wholesale tax permit."
- **Registration portal:** [OkTAP - Oklahoma Taxpayer Access Point](https://oktap.tax.ok.gov/oktap/Web/_/)
- **Registration fees:** $20 plus a handling fee

Sources: [Oklahoma New Business Center](https://oklahoma.gov/tax/businesses/new-business-center.html), [OkTAP](https://oktap.tax.ok.gov/oktap/Web/_/)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Oklahoma sales tax is levied at 4.5% on gross receipts from the sale or rental of tangible personal property.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food and food ingredients:** Exempt from state sales tax (effective August 29, 2024, under HB 1955). However, local sales and use taxes still apply to food. Notably, Oklahoma's definition of "food and food ingredients" includes candy, bottled soft drinks, and bottled water — all of which qualify for the state exemption. This is broader than many other states that exclude candy and soft drinks from food exemptions. Prepared food, alcoholic beverages, and dietary supplements remain subject to the full 4.5% state rate.
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable; no general clothing exemption.
  - **Prescription drugs and medicine:** Exempt from sales tax.
  - **Agricultural equipment and items for educational use:** Various exemptions apply.
- **Official exemptions information:** [Oklahoma Sales Tax Exemption Packet (PDF)](https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/tax/documents/forms/businesses/general/Packet-E.pdf), [State Sales Tax on Food and Food Ingredients](https://oklahoma.gov/tax/businesses/state-sales-tax-on-food-and-food-ingredients.html)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Both cities and counties levy additional sales taxes.
- **Range of local rates:** County rates range from 0.2% to 2%; city rates range from 1% to 5%. Combined local additions can bring the total rate up to 11.5%.
- **Do remote sellers collect local tax?** Yes. All sellers, regardless of location, are required to collect local sales and use taxes based on where the customer receives the taxable product or service (destination-based sourcing).

Sources: [Oklahoma Sales and Use Tax](https://oklahoma.gov/tax/businesses/sales-use-tax.html), [Oklahoma Rate Charts (PDF)](https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/tax/documents/resources/publications/businesses/sales-and-use-tax/rate-charts-copos/2026/copo1Q26.pdf)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Returns are generally filed monthly. Filing frequency may vary based on tax liability.
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns must be postmarked on or before the 20th day of the month following each reporting period. Returns must be filed for every period even if no tax is due.
- **Filing portal:** [OkTAP - Oklahoma Taxpayer Access Point](https://oktap.tax.ok.gov/oktap/Web/_/)

Sources: [Oklahoma Sales and Use Tax](https://oklahoma.gov/tax/businesses/sales-use-tax.html)
