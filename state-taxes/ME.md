# Maine (ME) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 5.5% (8% on prepared food and liquor served on-premises; higher rates apply to certain short-term rentals)
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 5.5% (Maine does not currently have broadly applied local sales taxes)

Source: [Sales and Use Tax Rates & Due Dates](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax/rates-due-dates)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales from the delivery of tangible personal property, products transferred electronically, or taxable services into Maine
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective January 1, 2022, via HP 891)
- **Measurement period:** Previous or current calendar year
- **Effective date:** July 1, 2018 (enforced following the Wayfair decision; codified in Maine Revised Statutes Title 36, Section 1951-B)
- **Official guidance:** [Guidance for Remote Sellers](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax/guidance-documents/remote-sellers)

Once a remote seller exceeds the threshold, they must register with Maine Revenue Services and begin collecting tax on or before the first day of the first month that begins at least 30 days after exceeding the threshold.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Maine Tax Portal (MTP). Click "Register a New Business" on the portal homepage and follow the prompts.
- **Registration portal:** [Maine Tax Portal](https://portal.maine.gov/salestax/welcome) | [Registration Page](https://portal.maine.gov/salestax/registration)
- **Registration fee:** No fee noted for sales tax registration
- **Retailer certificate:** A retailer certificate is issued upon registration, displaying the sales tax registration number.
- **Additional info:** [Sales, Use & Service Provider Tax](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax) | [Sales Tax FAQ](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/faq/sales-use-service-provider-tax)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Maine imposes a 5.5% sales tax on retail sales of tangible personal property and certain services.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Grocery staples:** Exempt. "Grocery staples" means food products ordinarily consumed for human nourishment (fruit, vegetables, fish, meat, dairy, bread, breakfast cereal, canned and boxed food products, condiments). Prepared food is taxable at 8%.
  - **Clothing:** Taxable at the full 5.5% rate (no exemption)
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt from sales tax
- **Official information:** [Sales, Use & Service Provider Tax](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax) | [Business Guide to Sales Tax (PDF)](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/sites/maine.gov.revenue/files/inline-files/BusinessGuide10162023_FINAL.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can municipalities add local sales tax?** Maine law allows municipalities to impose a local option sales tax if approved by voter referendum. However, local sales taxes are not broadly applied across the state.
- **Range of local rates:** Local option sales taxes are not currently widespread; the standard 5.5% state rate is the applicable rate in most jurisdictions.
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Generally not applicable since local sales taxes are not broadly in effect.

Source: [Sales, Use & Service Provider Tax](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Based on average monthly tax liability:
  - **$600+ per month:** Monthly filing
  - **$100 to $600 per month:** May file quarterly
  - **Less than $100 per month (but more than $50/year):** May file semiannually
  - **Less than $50 per year:** May file annually
- **Filing deadline:** Returns are due on the **15th of the month** following the end of the reporting period. If the 15th falls on a weekend or holiday, the return is due the next business day.
- **Filing portal:** [Maine Tax Portal](https://revenue.maine.gov/) | [About the Maine Tax Portal](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/portal)
- **Mailing address (if applicable):** Maine Revenue Services, P.O. Box 1065, Augusta, ME 04332-1065

Source: [Sales and Use Tax Rates & Due Dates](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/sales-use-service-provider-tax/rates-due-dates)
