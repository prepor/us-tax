# New Jersey (NJ) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does New Jersey have a sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.625%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6.625% (New Jersey does not have local sales taxes added on top of the state rate; however, Urban Enterprise Zones have a reduced rate of 3.3125% for qualifying in-person sales only)

Source: [NJ Division of Taxation - Sales and Use Tax](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/taxation/businesses/salestax/index.shtml)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 or more in gross revenue from sales of tangible personal property, specified digital products, or taxable services delivered into New Jersey during the current or prior calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** 200 or more separate transactions delivered into New Jersey during the current or prior calendar year
- **When the law took effect:** November 1, 2018
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/taxation/remotesellers.shtml)

A remote seller that does not meet either threshold is not required to register with the Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services for sales tax purposes.

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:**
  - **For remote sellers:** Go to the Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services website and choose the option to "Register as Remote Seller Only."
  - **Alternative:** Register through the [Streamlined Sales Tax Registration System](https://www.streamlinedsalestax.org/) for all Streamlined member states in one application.
  - **General business registration:** Complete Form NJ-REG (Business Registration Application) with the Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services at least 15 days before engaging in business activity.
- **Official registration portal:** [Register for Taxes - Business.NJ.gov](https://business.nj.gov/pages/register-for-taxes)
- **Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services:** [https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/gettingregistered.shtml](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/gettingregistered.shtml)
- **Registration fees:** No specific registration fee is mentioned on the official website for sales tax registration. *Note: Could not confirm whether a fee applies from official sources; contact the Division directly to verify.*

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. New Jersey assesses a 6.625% Sales Tax on sales of most tangible personal property, specified digital products, and certain services unless specifically exempt.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Clothing and footwear:** Exempt from sales tax (but clothing accessories such as handbags, jewelry, watches, umbrellas, and wigs are taxable)
  - **Food for home consumption (groceries):** Exempt from sales tax (but prepared food is taxable)
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax
  - **Over-the-counter drugs:** Exempt from sales tax
  - **Sports/recreational equipment:** Taxable (including cleated shoes, gloves, goggles, etc.)
  - **Costumes:** Exempt (but separately purchased masks and costume accessories are taxable)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No. New Jersey has a uniform statewide sales tax rate of 6.625%. There are no additional local sales taxes.
- **Urban Enterprise Zones (UEZ):** Qualified businesses located within designated Urban Enterprise Zones may charge a reduced rate of 3.3125% (50% of the standard rate) on qualifying in-person sales. This reduced rate does **not** apply to remote/online sales - it requires the transaction to physically occur at the business's location within the zone.
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** No local tax applies. Remote sellers collect the standard 6.625% statewide rate.

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** Required if you collected more than $30,000 in Sales and Use Tax in the preceding calendar year. Monthly payments are due, along with quarterly returns.
  - **Quarterly:** If you collected $30,000 or less, you file quarterly returns only (no monthly vouchers needed for the first or second month of each quarter).
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due by 11:59 p.m. on the **20th day of the month** following the end of the filing period. If the 20th falls on a weekend or holiday, the due date is the next business day.
