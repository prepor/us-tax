# North Carolina (NC) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 4.75% ([Current Sales and Use Tax Rates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-rates/current-sales-and-use-tax-rates))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Approximately 7.00%. Combined rates range from 6.75% to 7.50% depending on county ([Sales and Use Tax Rates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-rates))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross sales sourced to North Carolina in the previous or current calendar year ([Remote Sales - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/remote-sales))
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was repealed effective July 1, 2024, by House Bill 228) ([SD-24-1 - Repeal of Transaction Thresholds](https://www.ncdor.gov/sd-24-1-repeal-transaction-thresholdpdf/open))
- **Effective date:** November 1, 2018 (original economic nexus requirement); transaction threshold repealed July 1, 2024 ([NCDOR Press Release - Remote Sellers](https://www.ncdor.gov/news/press-releases/2018/08/08/ncdor-require-certain-remote-sellers-collect-sales-tax))
- **Official nexus guidance:** [Remote Sales - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/remote-sales)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Two options:
  1. **Streamlined Sales Tax Registration:** Register with all 24 SST member states (including NC) through one online application at [www.sstregister.org](https://www.sstregister.org) -- no fee ([FAQ - Remote Sales - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/remote-sales/frequently-asked-questions-remote-sales))
  2. **Direct NC registration:** Use the Department's online business registration portal or submit Form NC-BR ([Sales and Use Tax Registration - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-registration))
- **Registration portal:** [Business Registration - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/registration)
- **Registration fees:** No fee to apply for a certificate of registration ([FAQ - Remote Sales - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/remote-sales/frequently-asked-questions-remote-sales))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Tangible personal property sold at retail is generally subject to sales and use tax ([Sales and Use Tax - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/groceries:** Food for home consumption (qualifying food as defined by federal food stamp guidelines) is exempt from the 4.75% state sales tax. The only tax that applies is a flat 2.00% statewide local rate, meaning the total tax on qualifying food is 2.00% regardless of county (not 2.00% on top of the state rate). Items like candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, and prepared food do not qualify and are taxed at the full combined rate ([Food, Non-Qualifying Food, and Prepaid Meal Plans - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/food-non-qualifying-food-and-prepaid-meal-plans))
  - **Clothing:** Generally taxable at the full rate. There is an annual sales tax holiday (first weekend in August) where clothing, footwear, and school supplies at $100 or less per item are exempt ([Sale and Purchase Exemptions - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sale-and-purchase-exemptions))
  - **Prescription medicine:** Drugs dispensed pursuant to a prescription are exempt. Over-the-counter drugs are generally taxable ([Sale and Purchase Exemptions - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sale-and-purchase-exemptions))
- **Official exemption list:** [Sale and Purchase Exemptions - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sale-and-purchase-exemptions)

## Local Taxes
- **Can counties add local sales tax?** Yes. All 100 counties levy additional local sales and use taxes ([Current Sales and Use Tax Rates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-rates/current-sales-and-use-tax-rates))
- **Range of local rates:** 2.00% to 2.75%, resulting in combined rates of 6.75% to 7.50% ([Current Sales and Use Tax Rates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-rates/current-sales-and-use-tax-rates))
- **Remote seller local tax collection:** Yes. Remote sellers must collect both state and applicable county/local taxes based on the destination of the shipment ([Remote Sales - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/remote-sales))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Monthly, monthly with prepayment, quarterly, or annually, based on tax liability:
  - Monthly: tax liability consistently more than $100 but less than $20,000/month
  - Monthly with prepayment: tax liability of $20,000+ per month
  - Quarterly or annually: lower-volume filers
  ([Filing Frequency and Due Dates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-filing-requirements-payment-options/filing-frequency-and-due-dates))
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due on or before the **20th day of the month** following the end of the reporting period ([Filing Frequency and Due Dates - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/sales-and-use-tax-filing-requirements-payment-options/filing-frequency-and-due-dates))
- **Filing portal:** [E-File and Pay Your Sales and Use Tax Online - NCDOR](https://www.ncdor.gov/taxes-forms/sales-and-use-tax/efile-and-pay-your-sales-and-use-tax)
