# Rhode Island (RI) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 7%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 7% (Rhode Island does not have local sales taxes, so the rate is uniform statewide)

Sources: [Rhode Island Sales & Use Tax](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise-taxes/sales-use-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in gross revenue from sales delivered into Rhode Island in the previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (the 200-transaction threshold was eliminated effective July 1, 2023; only the $100,000 revenue threshold applies)
- **When the law took effect:** July 1, 2019 (in response to the U.S. Supreme Court's decision in South Dakota v. Wayfair, Inc.); transaction threshold repealed July 1, 2023
- **Official nexus information:** [Remote Sellers - RI Division of Taxation](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise/remote-sellers)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register online through the Rhode Island Division of Taxation's Business Application and Registration (BAR) system, or through the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Governing Board website.
- **Registration portal:** [RI Business Registration (BAR)](https://www.ri.gov/taxation/BAR/), [Register Your Business Online](https://tax.ri.gov/online-services/register-your-business-online)
- **Registration fees:** No registration fee for a sales tax permit. Permits must be renewed annually on or before February 1 of each year (no renewal fee).
- **Contact for registration questions:** RI Division of Taxation Registration Section at 401-574-8938

Sources: [RI Business Registration](https://www.ri.gov/taxation/BAR/), [Registration - RI Division of Taxation](https://tax.ri.gov/resources/businesses/registration)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Rhode Island imposes a 7% sales and use tax on sales of tangible personal property and certain services.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food and food ingredients (groceries):** Exempt. Food sold for human consumption (not including candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, alcoholic beverages, or prepared food) is exempt.
  - **Clothing:** Exempt for individual items priced at $250 or less. For items priced over $250, tax is calculated on the amount exceeding $250.
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt. Pharmaceuticals sold on prescription are exempt. Medical oxygen and insulin are exempt whether or not sold on prescription.
  - **Over-the-counter drugs:** Taxable (unless sold on prescription).
  - **Dietary supplements:** Taxable (unless sold on prescription).
- **Official exemptions information:** [Sales & Use Tax - RI Division of Taxation](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise-taxes/sales-use-tax), [Drugs, Medicines, and Health Care Products Regulation](https://rules.sos.ri.gov/Regulations/part/280-20-70-47), [RI Sales Tax Exemptions Advisory (PDF)](https://tax.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur541/files/2022-09/ADV_2022_21_sales_tax_exemptions.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Do cities/counties have local sales tax?** No. Rhode Island has a uniform statewide 7% sales and use tax rate. There are no additional local sales taxes imposed by cities or counties.
- **Remote seller impact:** Remote sellers only need to collect and remit the single statewide 7% rate.

Sources: [Sales & Use Tax - RI Division of Taxation](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise-taxes/sales-use-tax), [Remote Sellers](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise/remote-sellers)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Generally monthly. The Division of Taxation may assign a different filing frequency based on tax liability.
- **Filing deadlines:** Monthly returns are due on or before the 20th day of the month following the reporting period.
- **Annual reconciliation:** An annual sales tax reconciliation may also be required.
- **Late payment penalty:** 10% of the tax due, plus interest at the applicable rate (not less than 12%).
- **Filing portal:** [RI Tax Portal](https://tax.ri.gov/online-services/tax-portal) (accepts ACH Debit payments)

Sources: [Sales & Use Tax - RI Division of Taxation](https://tax.ri.gov/tax-sections/sales-excise-taxes/sales-use-tax), [RI Tax Portal](https://tax.ri.gov/online-services/tax-portal)
