# Idaho (ID) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does the state have sales tax?** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 6.0% ([Sales and Use - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/))
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 6.0% for most of Idaho. Some resort cities impose an additional local sales tax (up to 3%), but these are limited in scope and geography. ([Local Sales Tax - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/sales-tax/local-sales-tax/))

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in sales into Idaho in the current or previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None. Idaho uses only a revenue-based threshold, not a transaction count.
- **When the law took effect:** June 1, 2019
- **Official nexus information:** [Online Sellers Guide - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/guides-for-certain-groups/online-sellers/online-guide/)
- **Additional details:** [Explanation of Terms - Online Sellers - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/guides-for-certain-groups/online-sellers/explanation-of-terms/)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Register through the Idaho Business Registration (IBR) process to obtain a Seller's Permit ([Getting Tax Permits - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/online-services/business-registration/))
- **Online registration portal:** [Idaho Business Registration (IBR)](https://business.idaho.gov/) via [Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/online-services/business-registration/)
- **Registration fee:** No fee for a regular seller's permit ([Regular Seller's Permit - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/permits/permits/))
- **Note:** If you sell only through registered marketplace facilitators, you do not need your own Idaho seller's permit. ([Who Needs a Seller's Permit? - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/permits/who-needs-a-sellers-permit/))

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally taxable. ([Sales and Use Taxes: Basics Guide - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/online-guide/))
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Taxable. Idaho taxes groceries at the full 6% state rate. However, Idaho offers a grocery tax credit on income tax returns ($155 per person, or up to $250 with receipts) to offset this. ([Idaho Food Tax Credit - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/income-tax/individual-income/popular-credits-and-deductions/idaho-grocery-credit/))
  - **Clothing:** Taxable. No exemption for clothing.
  - **Prescription medicine:** Exempt. Prescription drugs are exempt from Idaho sales tax. ([Sales Tax Exemptions - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/exemptions/))
  - **SNAP/WIC purchases:** Exempt from sales tax ([Exempt Sales - Food, Meals and Drinks - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/guides-for-certain-groups/food/food-drinks/exempt-sales/))
- **Official exemption list:** [Sales Tax Exemptions - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/exemptions/)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities add local sales tax?** Yes, but only designated "resort cities" may impose a local sales tax. ([City Sales Taxes - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/sales-tax/local-sales-tax/city-sales-tax/))
- **Range of local rates:** Resort city local option taxes can range from 1% to 3%, depending on the city and voter-approved measures.
- **Remote seller collection:** Local resort city taxes are administered by the cities themselves, not by the Idaho State Tax Commission. Remote sellers generally collect only the state 6% rate. For specific resort city obligations, contact the city directly. ([Local Sales Tax - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/sales-tax/local-sales-tax/))

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:** Based on the amount of sales tax collected. Options include monthly, quarterly, or annual filing. The Idaho State Tax Commission assigns your filing frequency when you register. ([Sales Tax: Filing and Paying - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/stfiling/))
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns are due by the 20th of the month following the end of the reporting period (e.g., monthly return for January is due February 20). ([Sales Tax: Filing and Paying - Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/stfiling/))
- **Filing portal:** [Taxpayer Access Point (TAP)](https://tax.idaho.gov/online-services/) via [Idaho State Tax Commission](https://tax.idaho.gov/taxes/sales-use/stfiling/)
- **Note:** You must file a return even if you had no sales during the period.
