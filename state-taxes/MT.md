# Montana (MT) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Does Montana have a general sales tax?** No
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 0% (no general sales tax)
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** 0% (no general sales tax)

Montana is one of five U.S. states with no general sales tax. Online retailers selling physical goods into Montana are **not required** to collect sales tax.

Source: [Montana Department of Revenue - Sales Tax Guidance](https://revenue.mt.gov/taxes/general-sales-tax)

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Not applicable.** Montana does not impose a general sales or use tax, so there are no economic nexus thresholds for sales tax collection.

Source: [Montana Department of Revenue - Taxes](https://revenue.mt.gov/taxes/)

## Tax Registration
- **Not applicable.** Since Montana has no general sales tax, remote sellers do not need to register for a sales tax permit.
- There is no sales tax registration portal for general retail sales.

Source: [Montana Department of Revenue - Online Services](https://revenue.mt.gov/online-services/)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** No. Montana does not impose a general sales tax on tangible personal property.
- There are no general sales tax exemptions to list because no general sales tax exists.

**Note:** Montana does have some limited, specific taxes:
- **Lodging Facility Sales and Use Tax (4%):** Applies to hotels, motels, bed and breakfasts, guest ranches, resorts, and campgrounds. This does not apply to online retail sales of physical goods.
- **Local Resort Tax (up to 3%):** Certain resort communities (Big Sky, Cooke City, Craig, Gardiner, Red Lodge, St. Regis, Virginia City, West Yellowstone, Whitefish, and Wolf Creek) may impose a local resort tax on goods and services sold within those areas. This applies only to in-person sales within the designated resort community/area and would not apply to remote online sales.

Sources:
- [Lodging Facility Sales and Use Tax](https://revenue.mt.gov/taxes/miscellaneous/lodging-facility)
- [Local Resort Tax](https://revenue.mt.gov/taxes/miscellaneous/local-resort-tax)
- [Montana Department of Commerce - Resort Tax Analysis](https://commerce.mt.gov/Data-Research/Resort-Tax-Analysis)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** No general local sales tax exists. The only local tax mechanism is the resort tax (up to 3%) in designated resort communities, which is approved by local voters and applies to sales within those areas.
- **Range of local rates:** 0% to 3% (resort tax only, in limited communities)
- **Do remote sellers need to collect local tax?** No. The resort tax applies to sales occurring within the designated resort community/area and does not apply to remote online sales.

Source: [Local Resort Tax](https://revenue.mt.gov/taxes/miscellaneous/local-resort-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Not applicable** for general sales tax. There are no sales tax returns to file for online retail sales into Montana.
