# Colorado (CO) - Sales Tax Guide for Online Retailers

## Overview
- **Has sales tax:** Yes
- **State-level sales tax rate:** 2.9%
- **Combined average rate (state + local):** Varies significantly by jurisdiction. The state rate of 2.9% is supplemented by various local taxes. Combined rates can range from 2.9% (state only) to over 11% in some jurisdictions. Use the [DR 1002 publication](https://tax.colorado.gov/DR1002) to look up specific rates.

## Economic Nexus Thresholds
- **Revenue threshold:** $100,000 in retail sales into Colorado in the current or previous calendar year
- **Transaction threshold:** None (Colorado uses only a revenue threshold)
- **When the law took effect:** The economic nexus requirement was established through [HB 19-1240](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb19-1240) and subsequent legislation including [SB 20-099](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb20-099)
- **Key details:** Retailers must apply for a sales tax license and begin collecting Colorado sales tax by the first day of the first month commencing at least 90 days after their aggregate Colorado sales in the current year exceed $100,000
- **Official nexus information:** [Out-of-State Businesses](https://tax.colorado.gov/out-of-state-businesses)

## Tax Registration
- **How to register:** Apply using the Sales Tax / Wage Withholding Account Application (CR 0100) through Revenue Online
- **Registration portal:** [How to Apply for a Colorado Sales Tax License](https://tax.colorado.gov/how-to-apply-for-a-colorado-sales-tax-license)
- **Registration fees:** For in-state businesses: $16 license fee (prorated depending on date of issuance) plus a $50 deposit at the time of application. Licenses are valid for a two-year period and expire at the end of each odd-numbered year; renewal fee is $16 per location. For remote sellers with no Colorado physical presence: the Retailer's Use Tax License is free with no deposit required.
- **More info:** [Sales Tax Accounts & Licenses](https://tax.colorado.gov/sales-tax-account-license)

## Taxable Goods
- **Are physical goods generally taxable?** Yes. Retail sales of tangible personal property are generally subject to Colorado sales tax.
- **Notable exemptions:**
  - **Food/Groceries:** Food purchased for home consumption (items that can be purchased with food stamps/WIC vouchers) is exempt from state sales tax. However, prepared food, hot food ready to eat, and food to be consumed on premises are taxable. Note: some local jurisdictions may still tax groceries.
  - **Clothing:** Clothing is generally **taxable** in Colorado (no general clothing exemption).
  - **Prescription drugs:** Exempt from sales tax.
  - **Over-the-counter medicine:** Vitamins, dietary supplements, and non-prescription medicines are generally **not exempt**.
- **Official exemption list:** [Sales Tax Exemptions & Deductions](https://tax.colorado.gov/sales-tax-exemptions-deductions)
- **Food-specific guidance:** [FYI Sales 4 - Taxable and Tax Exempt Sales of Food](https://tax.colorado.gov/sites/tax/files/Sales%204.pdf)

## Local Taxes
- **Can cities/counties add local sales tax?** Yes. Colorado has one of the most complex local sales tax systems in the country.
- **Range of local rates:** Local rates vary widely. Counties, municipalities, and special districts can all impose sales tax. Combined local rates can add several percentage points on top of the 2.9% state rate.
- **Home rule cities:** 72 municipalities and 2 counties "self-collect" their own local sales tax and may have different rules and exemptions. State-collected jurisdictions include approximately 150 municipalities, 50 counties, and various special districts.
- **Remote seller obligations:** Remote sellers must collect applicable state and state-administered local sales taxes. For self-collected (home-rule) jurisdictions, remote sellers may need to register separately — however, under [HB24-1041](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb24-1041) (effective January 1, 2025), home-rule jurisdictions that do not participate in SUTS can no longer require remote sellers (those with no physical presence in Colorado) to collect their local sales tax, unless the retailer voluntarily elects to do so. Colorado's [SUTS (Sales & Use Tax System)](https://tax.colorado.gov/SUTS-Jurisdictions) simplifies filing for participating jurisdictions.
- **More info:** [Local Government Sales Tax](https://tax.colorado.gov/local-government-sales-tax)

## Filing Requirements
- **Filing frequency:**
  - **Monthly:** Required if average/estimated monthly state sales tax collection is $600 or more
  - **Quarterly:** Required if average/estimated monthly state sales tax collection is less than $600
  - **Annually:** May be granted if average/estimated monthly sales tax collection is $15 or less
- **Filing deadlines:** Returns and payments are due by the **20th day of the month** following the close of the tax period. If the 20th falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, the return is due the next business day. Annual returns are due January 20.
- **Important:** You must file a return every filing period, even if no sales were made and no tax was collected.
- **Filing portal:** [File Sales Tax on Revenue Online](https://tax.colorado.gov/file-sales-tax-online)
- **Filing information:** [Sales Tax Filing Information](https://tax.colorado.gov/sales-tax-filing-information)
